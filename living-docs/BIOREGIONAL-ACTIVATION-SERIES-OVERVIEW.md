# Bridging Bioregional Wisdom in the Northeast of Turtle Island

**A Co-Created Bioregional Activation Series**

---

## What Is This?

The Bioregional Activation Series is a co-created gathering series emerging from the convergence of bioregional organizing, regenerative finance, land-based community building, governance education, and culture-making across the Northeast. Co-stewarded by Syd Harvey Griffith and Susanna Choe, the series is designed not as a conference or a program to be delivered -- but as a living process of listening, mapping, and weaving that prepares the Northeast bioregion for deeper coordination and shared resourcing.

The core question driving the series: **Who is already doing this work, what do they need, and how do we build the relational and structural foundations to resource a thriving Northeast bioregion together?**

## Why Now?

The regenerative movement in the Northeast is rich with practitioners, land stewards, organizers, technologists, funders, and culture bearers -- but much of this work remains siloed. People are arriving at the same conclusions independently, building overlapping infrastructure without visibility into each other's efforts. The series exists to surface these connections, build trust across them, and create the conditions for shared action -- without imposing a centralized structure.

As the United States approaches its 250th anniversary, the series also honors the Haudenosaunee Great Law of Peace, centering Indigenous governance wisdom that originated in the Northeast and influenced American democracy. This is a moment to reconnect bioregional governance with responsibility to place, reconciliation, and the living lineages of collective stewardship rooted in this land.

## What It Aims to Produce

The series is oriented toward five tangible outcomes, each emerging through relationship rather than top-down design:

1. **A Northeast Bioregional Flow Fund** -- a trust-based, participatory funding mechanism rooted in relationship and local stewardship, where communities closest to the work decide where capital flows.

2. **A Northeast Bioregional Currency / Token** -- a practical coordination and exchange tool with governance layers explored when the bioregion is ready, reducing dependency on extractive financial systems.

3. **Development and fundraising capacity for Northeast land-based hubs** -- strengthening the bioregional finance facilities, farms, community centers, and project sites already doing the work.

4. **Commitment pooling across the bioregion** -- coordinating time, skills, relationships, land access, and funding to strengthen collaboration and mutual aid.

5. **A Northeast bioregional festival** -- a leave-a-positive-trace celebration co-created through the Planetary Party Protocol, where every gathering leaves lasting regenerative infrastructure.

## The Pillars

The series is organized around six interwoven pillars:

**Relationship-Building & Mapping** is the foundation. Before building anything, the series asks: who is already stewarding land, capital, governance, culture, or exchange? Where are the natural synergies? This pillar focuses on coherence, not speed -- ensuring that whatever structures emerge are rooted in trust and shared understanding.

**Governance Education** is woven throughout as living infrastructure -- shared language around decision-making, exposure to participatory and commons-based governance models, and practical support for co-creating across difference without fragmentation.

**Tools, Tech & Frameworks** surfaces the technologies and coordination platforms already supporting the movement -- mapping tools, participatory governance frameworks, regenerative finance infrastructure, storytelling platforms -- and invites the builders into the room so the bioregion can see what's available, what's interoperable, and what's missing.

**Healing & Conflict Transformation** is made available responsively, not centered. Every community carries friction and unresolved tensions that can block collaboration. Skilled facilitators hold space for acknowledgment and integration without derailing the broader work.

**Honoring Place** grounds the series in the governance wisdom of the land itself -- the Great Law of Peace, Indigenous stewardship traditions, and the living ecologies of the Northeast bioregion.

**Culture, Arts & Creative Expression** invites music, visual art, storytelling, and ceremony as forms of cultural infrastructure -- supporting shared identity, celebration, and imaginative vision alongside the structural work.

## How It Unfolds

The series moves through three phases:

**Online preparation sessions** introduce the purpose, orient participants to shared language and bioregional context, and invite advance mapping so a living picture of the field already exists when people gather in person.

**In-person gatherings at anchor sites** like Hudson Harmonic create space for embodied relationship-building, sensemaking, governance practice, storytelling, and design labs -- translating mapped insights into lived connection and next steps.

**Distributed bioregional hub gatherings** extend the work to partner-hosted activations at farms, community centers, and land-based hubs across the Northeast -- locally relevant expressions connected back to shared learning and coordination.

## Resourcing

Kinship Earth has contributed $7,500 in seed funding to launch the series, aligned with its mission to help bioregional organizing groups prepare for and launch their own Flow Funding foundations. Kinship Earth is also raising matching funds to further resource facilitators, organizers, artists, contributors, tools, mapping, and coordination infrastructure.

The series features contributions from technology innovators (Spatial Network, Catalist, Social Roots, Hylo, Bloom Network) alongside land-based communities (True North, Hudson Harmonic, Wally Farms) and the Bioregional Embassy, which provides spatial intelligence and diplomatic agency.

## How the Series Intersects with Syd's Ecosystem

The Bioregional Activation Series is not a standalone project -- it is the place where all of Syd's initiatives converge in practice. Each initiative plays a distinct role in making the series possible and is, in turn, strengthened by it:

**Kinship Earth** provides the financial backbone and the Flow Funding methodology that the series is preparing the Northeast to adopt. The series is a direct expression of Kinship Earth's mission: helping bioregions build the relational and structural readiness to launch their own trust-based Flow Funds. Every relationship built and every map drawn through the series feeds Kinship Earth's broader field-building work.

**Planetary Party Protocol** provides the coordination architecture. The series follows the Protocol's five-phase rhythm -- Sense, See, Flow, Celebrate, Regenerate -- and its guild structure offers the functional scaffolding (governance, storytelling, bioregional intelligence, tech, capital flows) through which participants can contribute. The Northeast festival envisioned as an outcome of the series is a direct Planetary Party activation.

**Permatours** is the ground-level network that makes the series real. With 50+ events across 40+ land-based project sites in the Northeast, Permatours already holds the relationships, the hubs, and the mutual aid infrastructure that the series builds upon. The distributed hub gatherings are hosted through the Permatours network. The series accelerates Permatours' evolution from a permaculture action network into a full bioregional mutual aid and coordination body.

**Unified Sanctuaries** represents what the series is ultimately building toward -- place-based regenerative community rooted in land. As one hub among many, it can host gatherings and serve as a living laboratory for the governance, tokenomics, and cooperative models being explored through the series. The open-source blueprint Unified Sanctuaries is developing becomes a resource for every land-based hub the series surfaces.

**Live with Syd** is the storytelling and community-cultivation engine. Weekly sessions across all three themes (Monday capital flows, Wednesday coordination, Friday land development) create the ongoing relational container that sustains the series between gatherings. The content pipeline generated through the sessions feeds fundraising, visibility, and coherence across the entire effort.

**Women of the Turning** brings the feminine leadership, somatic wisdom, and ceremonial depth that shapes how the series convenes. The circle's emphasis on coherence, attunement, and infrastructure rooted in care directly informs the series' approach to healing, conflict transformation, and governance as relational practice.

In short: the Bioregional Activation Series is the living demonstration that these initiatives are not separate projects running in parallel -- they are facets of a single, coherent movement toward bioregional sovereignty, regenerative economics, and community self-determination. The series is where the ecosystem meets the ground.
