# Land Acquisition Strategy: Syd & Eslerh

**A framework for acquiring and developing regenerative land in Mexico and the US**

*Living document -- last updated March 2026*

---

## Strategic Context

Syd and Eslerh want to root their life and work in land. Land serves multiple functions for them:
- **Home base** for their partnership and daily life
- **Production facility** for Micelio Media
- **Gathering space** for Planetary Party events and community programming
- **Demonstration site** for regenerative agriculture and land stewardship
- **Revenue generator** through events, retreats, agriculture, and media

They are considering properties in **Veracruz, Mexico** (Coatepec / Xalapa region) and **Vermont, USA**. Both locations have strong pull factors. The question is: which comes first, and how to sequence.

## Option Analysis

### Option A: Mexico First (Recommended)

**Location:** Coatepec / Xalapa region, Veracruz

**Why Mexico First**
- Significantly lower land costs -- $200K-$500K buys substantial property with infrastructure
- Eslerh's family and cultural roots in the region
- Strong regenerative agriculture potential (tropical/subtropical climate)
- Planetary Party gatherings in Mexico align with global movement positioning
- Lower cost of living during the building phase
- Growing regenerative community in the region
- Cultural richness and creative inspiration for Micelio Media

**Considerations**
- Foreign ownership rules: Mexicans can own land freely; foreigners face restrictions in certain zones. Since Eslerh is Mexican, property can be in his name or held through a Mexican entity. Consult a Mexican attorney.
- Infrastructure development may require local knowledge and relationships
- Distance from US-based philanthropic networks and funders
- Potential visa/residency considerations for Syd

**Financial Scenario: Mexico First**

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|---------------|
| Land + existing structures (1-5 hectares with house) | $80,000 - $200,000 |
| Renovation and basic infrastructure | $30,000 - $80,000 |
| Media studio setup | $15,000 - $30,000 |
| Regenerative agriculture initial planting | $10,000 - $25,000 |
| Gathering/event space development | $20,000 - $50,000 |
| Reserve fund (6 months living expenses) | $15,000 - $25,000 |
| **Total** | **$170,000 - $410,000** |

At the lower end, the $200K down payment covers a full acquisition and basic development in Mexico, with room remaining for reserves. At the higher end, the full $500K budget is used but results in a significantly developed property.

### Option B: Vermont / US First

**Location:** Southern Vermont (aligned with Unified Sanctuaries network)

**Why US First**
- Proximity to existing organizational infrastructure (Kinship Earth, Permatours)
- Familiar legal/financial systems
- Stronger short-term fundraising environment
- Unified Sanctuaries ecosystem and collaborator network
- Easier integration with US-based funders and partners

**Considerations**
- $200K-$500K buys significantly less property and infrastructure in the US
- Higher cost of living and development
- Less cultural alignment with Eslerh's roots
- Vermont winters limit year-round outdoor programming

**Financial Scenario: US First**

| Item | Estimated Cost |
|------|---------------|
| Land + existing structure (5-20 acres with house) | $250,000 - $450,000 |
| Renovation and infrastructure | $50,000 - $150,000 |
| Total needed | $300,000 - $600,000 |

This exceeds the current budget at most price points and would require mortgage financing for almost any scenario.

### Option C: Both Simultaneously

**Not recommended at this stage.** Managing two property acquisitions across two countries with current resources would spread capital too thin and create operational complexity. Better to establish one strong base first.

## Land Selection Criteria (Mexico)

When evaluating specific properties in the Coatepec/Xalapa region:

### Must-Haves
- [ ] Access to water (spring, well, or reliable municipal)
- [ ] Existing habitable structure or buildable site
- [ ] Road access (for events and deliveries)
- [ ] Clear title and ownership (consult attorney)
- [ ] Minimum 1 hectare for agriculture and gathering space
- [ ] Within 30 minutes of Xalapa or Coatepec (for services, airport access)

### Strong Preferences
- [ ] Existing fruit trees or mature vegetation
- [ ] Views or topography that support gathering and filming
- [ ] Quiet enough for media production
- [ ] Potential for multiple structures (residences, studio, gathering pavilion)
- [ ] Proximity to regenerative community or potential collaborators
- [ ] Internet connectivity (essential for remote work and streaming)

### Nice to Have
- [ ] Existing outbuildings or barns
- [ ] River or stream on property
- [ ] Historical or cultural significance
- [ ] Enough acreage for future expansion (3-5+ hectares)

## Legal and Structural Considerations

### Mexico Property Ownership
- **Mexican nationals** can own land freely anywhere in Mexico
- **Foreigners** face restrictions within 50km of coast and 100km of borders (restricted zone); Coatepec/Xalapa is inland and generally outside this zone
- Options for holding: Eslerh's name, joint ownership structure, Mexican LLC (Sociedad), or fideicomiso (bank trust for foreign ownership if needed)
- **Action item**: Consult Mexican real estate attorney on optimal ownership structure

### US Tax Implications
- US citizens (Syd) are taxed on worldwide income regardless of residence
- Foreign property ownership creates reporting obligations (FBAR, FATCA if applicable)
- Potential treaty benefits for Mexico-US
- **Action item**: Consult US tax advisor familiar with Mexico

### Immigration
- Syd in Mexico: US citizens can stay 180 days on tourist visa; longer stays require temporary or permanent residency
- Eslerh in US: Depends on current immigration status; consult attorney
- **Action item**: Immigration attorney consultation for both directions
