**Integrating personal finances, project revenue, and regenerative enterprise**

*Living document -- last updated March 2026*

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Separate personal and project finances clearly.** Syd's nonprofit work (Kinship Earth, Permatours) must maintain clean financial boundaries. Personal income comes from salaries, consulting, media revenue, and earned income -- not from redirecting philanthropic capital.
2. **Build toward financial resilience, not maximum extraction.** The goal is enough income to sustain a good life while investing in land, community, and creative work.
3. **Diversify income sources.** No single revenue stream should represent more than 40% of personal income.
4. **Invest in assets that appreciate and produce.** Land, skills, community relationships, and creative works all generate long-term value.
5. **As Eslerh's income grows, shared expenses shift toward equitable contribution.** Syd currently carries the household; this should rebalance as Eslerh earns more.

## Transitioning to Shared Expenses

As Eslerh's income grows, here's a graduated approach:

### Phase 1: Now (2026) -- Syd Carries, Eslerh Ramps
- Syd continues covering shared expenses
- Eslerh focuses on building KE contractor income and Micelio portfolio
- Eslerh covers his own personal expenses (phone, subscriptions, Paco)
- **Eslerh's target**: earn $15K-$25K from KE by end of 2026

### Phase 2: Earning (Late 2026 - 2027) -- Begin Sharing
- Once Eslerh earns $3K+/month reliably, begin contributing to shared expenses
- Start with a percentage split based on income ratio (e.g., 70/30 then moving toward 60/40)
- Eslerh covers groceries or a portion of housing costs
- **Trigger**: Eslerh's monthly income exceeds $3,000 consistently

### Phase 3: Partnership (2028+) -- Equitable Split
- Both contributing to shared expenses proportional to income
- Shared account for household expenses, individual accounts for personal
- Joint investment in land development and shared projects
