*Living document -- last updated March 2026*

---

## Guiding Principles

1. **Separate personal and project finances clearly.** Syd's nonprofit work (Kinship Earth, Permatours) must maintain clean financial boundaries. Personal income comes from salaries, consulting, media revenue, and earned income -- not from redirecting philanthropic capital.
2. **Build toward financial resilience, not maximum extraction.** The goal is enough income to sustain a good life while investing in land, community, and creative work.
3. **Diversify income sources.** No single revenue stream should represent more than 40% of personal income.
4. **Invest in assets that appreciate and produce.** Land, skills, community relationships, and creative works all generate long-term value.

## Micelio Media Business Model

Eslerh's media work needs a clear business structure to generate reliable income:

### Revenue Model Options

**Option 1: Contract Production Studio**
- Produce documentaries, event coverage, and storytelling for aligned organizations
- Clients: foundations, nonprofits, regenerative enterprises, movement organizations
- Pricing: $5,000-$25,000 per project depending on scope
- Target: 6-10 projects/year = $30,000-$250,000
- Pros: Clear revenue, leverages existing skills and network
- Cons: Client-dependent, less creative freedom

**Option 2: Content Creator / Platform**
- Build audience through social media, YouTube, newsletter
- Monetize through sponsorship, membership, licensing
- Pros: Creative freedom, scalable
- Cons: Slow build, unpredictable revenue

**Option 3: Hybrid (Recommended)**
- Contract work provides base income (60-70%)
- Platform/content provides growth income and audience (30-40%)
- Contract work also builds the content library and audience
- Land-based production reduces costs significantly

4. Set up simple business entity (Mexican or US depending on clients)
