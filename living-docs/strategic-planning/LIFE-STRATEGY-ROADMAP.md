## Annual Rhythm (Proposed)

A bi-national life requires intentional seasonal design:

| Season | Location | Focus |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **January - March** | Mexico (Veracruz region) | Land stewardship, Micelio Media production, Planetary Party Mexico planning, creative retreat |
| **April - May** | US (Northeast) | Kinship Earth fundraising season, Permatours spring events, US network cultivation |
| **June - August** | Flexible / Travel | Planetary Party gatherings, global movement participation, documentary filming |
| **September - October** | US (Vermont / Northeast) | Permatours fall events, harvest, Unified Sanctuaries work, US community engagement |
| **November - December** | Mexico | Dia de Muertos and cultural engagement, year-end reflection, strategic planning for next year |

This rhythm allows:
- Deep presence in Mexico during the most productive growing and cultural seasons
- US engagement during key fundraising and event windows
- Summer flexibility for global movement work
- Annual cycle of reflection and planning

## Strategic Questions to Revisit Quarterly

1. **Land**: Are we on track for acquisition? What's blocking us? Do we need to adjust our location strategy?
2. **Money**: Are we building toward financial resilience? Are project finances and personal finances clearly separated?
3. **Work Integration**: Is Eslerh's creative work well-supported within the ecosystem? Is Syd overextended?
4. **Partnership**: Are we maintaining balance between shared work and personal space? Are we taking care of each other?
5. **Community**: Are we building the community we want to live in? Are we over-committing to external obligations?
6. **Global Context**: Has anything changed in the world that requires us to adjust? (immigration policy, economic conditions, movement dynamics)

## Risk Factors

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Immigration/visa complications for bi-national living | Medium | High | Consult immigration attorney early; maintain flexibility on primary residence |
| Land acquisition takes longer than expected | Medium | Medium | Continue renting; don't over-commit to a timeline |
| Syd carrying all expenses long-term causes strain | High | High | Formalize Eslerh's KE contractor role immediately; graduated expense sharing as income grows |
| Project finances crowd out personal financial stability | High | High | Clear separation of personal and project accounts; protect $20K emergency fund |
| Overextension across too many projects | High | Medium | Prioritize ruthlessly; say no to opportunities that don't serve the 3-year plan |
| Key relationships or partnerships shift | Medium | Medium | Diversify dependencies; build institutional resilience |
| Global economic or political disruption | Medium | High | Land-based resilience; multiple currencies; community mutual aid |
