# Intro Video -- Introducing Athena Demos & Franz Josef Allmayer

**Format:** Syd-to-camera intro for Live with Syd or social clip
**Context:** Introducing two collaborators whose work intersects with the Planetary Party / Kinship Earth / Permatours / Unified Sanctuaries ecosystem

---

## Script

I want to introduce you to two people whose work I'm really excited about -- and more importantly, how what they're building connects to everything we're doing across our ecosystem.

First -- Athena Demos. Athena is the co-founder of Burnersphere, a social VR platform built for the Burning Man community. But what she's really doing is much bigger than that. Her team builds immersive virtual worlds where people can gather, experience spaces together, fundraise, and collaborate -- all without anyone flying anywhere or renting a venue.

She's done projects with Microsoft on social impact -- a Pride museum, research on sound navigation for blind users in VR, community storytelling. She's worked with architects to let them walk through their structures before they're built and catch design problems in real time. She helped Philippe Cousteau's team redesign an underwater research station during COVID -- engineers literally walked through the virtual model and realized they had the whole layout wrong. They flipped the building, removed unnecessary pressure points, and then hosted a fundraiser inside the model with people like James Cameron and Leonardo DiCaprio. All virtual. No physical event. They raised what they needed and built the thing.

What I love about Athena's work is her commitment to creating spaces that are genuinely free -- no surveillance, no data harvesting, no corporate terms of service mining your conversations. On Burnersphere, only the people in the world with you can hear you. That matters.

And she's going after social impact projects -- museums, the 2028 LA Olympics, connecting communities in VR so people who can't travel can still experience and participate. She's also exploring how regenerative and intentional communities could have a shared virtual space -- a place where all these different land-based projects could be represented, where you could portal between them, learn from each other, and become part of a collective. When she described that vision, I immediately thought of RegenCivics and the bioregional network we're weaving.

Now -- Franz Josef Allmayer. Franz brings the infrastructure layer. He's building tools for digital measurement, reporting, and verification -- DMRV -- along with digital twin technology, tokenomics frameworks, and carbon credit pathways. His work spans multiple bioregions and he's connected to institutional players, family offices, and EU-funded initiatives including work across an eight-nation Alpine partnership.

Where Franz's work meets ours is everywhere. Our Permatours network has 40-plus land-based hubs across the Northeast doing regenerative agriculture, natural building, food forests, ecological restoration -- generating real ecological value but lacking the data infrastructure to prove it. Franz's DMRV tools could help these communities document and verify what they're doing, pool their data, and access carbon finance. His tokenomics infrastructure could support the dual-token model we're developing for Permatours and the bioregional token work we're doing for the Northeast.

For Kinship Earth, Franz's digital infrastructure could help us build the donor-facing tools we need -- pooled fund vaults, verifiable impact credentials, transparent tracking that satisfies institutional funders while keeping the process trust-based and lightweight. For Unified Sanctuaries, his digital twin and governance tools could help us plan our village in the digital sphere before we build it physically.

Here's what's exciting to me -- Athena and Franz are working on complementary sides of the same vision. Athena builds the immersive worlds where communities can gather, visualize, and experience together. Franz builds the verification, governance, and financial infrastructure that makes those communities legible to funders and connected to real capital flows. Together with what we're building -- the bioregional networks, the flow funding, the land-based hubs, the activation series -- there's a full stack here. Communities on the ground doing the work. Digital twins and virtual spaces to visualize and connect. Infrastructure to verify impact and move resources.

That's the kind of collaboration I'm most excited about -- where the technology actually serves the communities, and the communities give the technology meaning.

So -- welcome, Athena and Franz. I'm grateful for both of you, and I'm looking forward to building this together.
