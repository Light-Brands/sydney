# **🌊 Water Blessing – “One Water, One Life”**

*(Invite everyone to hold their cup of water)*

As we step into this year together as Women of the Turning, let’s remember what makes leadership possible. Before there were economies, before there were institutions, before there were nations \- there was water.

Water moving. Water cycling. Water remembering. Water is the source of life.

Every cell in our bodies carries it. Every child born enters through it. Every seed requires it to awaken.

Without water, nothing flourishes.  
 Not soil. Not communities. Not imagination. Not peace.

Healthy waters create healthy soil. Healthy soil grows nourishing food.  
 Nourishing food builds strong bodies. Strong bodies support clear minds.  
 Clear minds make wise decisions. Wise decisions create thriving communities.

One Water. One Life.  There is no separation.

The water in your cup has been glacier, river, ocean, cloud.  
 It has moved through forests.  
 Through animals. Through ancestors.  
 Through tears. Through milk. Through rain.

It carries memory. It carries future. It carries us.

Let’s take a moment now.   Hold your water in your hands.

Feel its temperature. Feel its weight. Feel its quiet presence.

This water is older than we are. It will outlive us.  
 It does not belong to us. We belong to it.

As women gathering at this turning of the age,  
 we remember that to steward life is to steward water.

To protect water is to protect food.  
 To protect food is to protect children.  
 To protect children is to protect the future.

Water teaches us how to lead.

She does not dominate. She nourishes.  
 She shapes stone not through force, but through persistence and coherence.

She moves around obstacles. She joins with other waters.  
 She becomes powerful not alone, but together.

As you hold your water, whisper into it one intention you carry for this community of women.

Let the water receive it.   (Pause.)

Now imagine that every woman here is holding water.  
 Different cups. Different lands. Different watersheds.

And yet \- it is One Water.   One Life.

When you are ready, place a small drop of your water onto the Earth,  
 into a bowl, onto a plant, or simply back into your cup \-  
 as a gesture of returning your intention to the greater whole.

May our leadership be like water. Life-giving. Persistent. Coherent.  
 In service to all life.

One Water. One Life.

And so it is.
