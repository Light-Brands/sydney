# Planetary Party Protocol: Governance Council Proposal

**Date:** March 6, 2026
**Status:** Proposal for Team Review
**Source:** PPP Meeting (March 2026) + Nadim Hamdan's Structural Recommendations

---

## Summary

This proposal establishes a **Small Council** (Anchor Circle / Coherence Council) as the operational governance body of the Planetary Party Protocol, replacing the current single large-group weekly call with a structured, role-clear system that supports both coordination and relational trust.

---

## Problem Statement

The current format (one 40-minute weekly meeting with 8+ participants) lacks the structural coherence needed for effective self-management. Key issues identified:

- **Debate-heavy calls** reduce psychological safety and eat time without resolving tensions
- **No clear coordinator role** means follow-ups, decisions, and accountability fall through the cracks
- **Team size (8+)** exceeds agile best practice (3-7) for a single self-managed operational group
- **Cohesion exists; coherence does not** -- shared intention is strong, but roles, decision-making processes, and shared language remain undefined

---

## Proposal: Small Council + Weekly Community Circle

### 1. Small Council (Anchor Circle / Coherence Council)

A core group of 3-7 members with operational capacity and appetite to hold the coordination function. The Council meets regularly across three distinct meeting types:

| Meeting Type | Purpose | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| **Tactical** | Move work forward, clarify next actions, track projects and deliverables | Weekly |
| **Governance** | Evolve roles, policies, structure; make decisions on funding distribution | Bi-weekly or as needed |
| **Heart Sharing** | Maintain emotional, relational, and spiritual connection; tend to nervous system safety | Weekly or bi-weekly |

**Open to everyone** -- any ecosystem participant may attend any Small Council meeting.

### 2. Weekly Community Check-In (All Hands)

Everyone in the broader ecosystem shares a **brief update once per week** on the work done in their circle or role. This can happen:

- **Live** on the weekly community call (1 minute per person max), or
- **Async** in the designated communications channel (e.g., Slack) if unable to attend live

This replaces the current format where the full group attempts operational governance together.

---

## Meeting Agreements

For all check-in calls:

- **Arrive on time** -- best effort commitment from all participants
- **Check-ins: 1 minute each** -- concise, focused updates
- **Calls capped at 45 minutes or less** -- if a topic needs more time, move it to the appropriate communications channel or schedule a focused session
- If a participant cannot share their check-in live, they post it async in the comms channel

---

## Decision-Making

The Small Council uses **consent-based / integrative decision-making** (not consensus, not debate):

- Proposals are prepared in advance where possible
- Decisions proceed unless there is a **principled objection** (not just a preference)
- The **advice method** is used for operational decisions: seek input from those affected, then decide and communicate clearly

---

## Key Structural Clarifications (per Nadim's Recommendations)

1. **Language is infrastructure** -- establish a shared glossary of terms (protocol, guild, working group, circle, etc.) with clear definitions
2. **Groups coordinate; individuals perform work** -- accountability lives with named people in named roles, not abstract "working groups"
3. **Psychological safety is non-negotiable** -- meeting structures must support nervous system safety, not undermine it
4. **Coherence over cohesion** -- shared language, clear roles, explicit agreements about how decisions get made

---

## Operational Principle: Circle Lead Authority

Each circle lead (or role holder) must be empowered to **follow through on their work even when others in the ecosystem aren't yet ready or available to offer input**. Specifically:

- A circle lead can **onboard partners**, **share financials with prospective funders**, and **follow up on funding conversations** within the scope of their role without waiting for full-group consensus
- If an entity (e.g., Nalawaya, Bioregional Embassy, Kinship Earth) has official partners, those partners are recognized as part of the planetary party ecosystem through that entity's participation
- The advice method applies: seek input from those affected, but do not block forward motion on time-sensitive opportunities (e.g., funder follow-ups, activation planning, partnership formalization)
- Agreements and MOUs between ecosystem entities clarify the boundaries of each circle's authority

This addresses the tension surfaced on the call: capacity to move on funder relationships and partner onboarding should not be held up by the group's readiness to articulate every detail collectively.

## Next Steps

### Structural Decisions
1. **Team decision:** Adopt this Small Council model (or choose the alternative: appoint a single Project Manager with clear mandate, authority, and compensation)
2. **Identify Small Council members** -- who has the capacity and appetite to serve?
3. **Set meeting cadence** and begin first Tactical + Heart Sharing sessions

### Language & Coherence
4. **Create a shared glossary / lexicon** -- define key terms (protocol, guild, working group, circle, trust, federation, etc.) with clear meanings so the ecosystem speaks a common language; include origin/context of terms where useful for onboarding
5. **Align on standard language** where possible with recognized frameworks (agile, commons governance, bioregional organizing) so external partners and funders can easily orient -- per Nadim's point that language signals credibility and transmits culture
6. **Refine the Theory of Change** -- translate the aligned Column B/C content from the meeting spreadsheet into a unified, living document that each entity can reference

### Communications & Transparency
7. **Establish communications platform** (Slack) with transparent channels per project/pod -- each channel visible to all but participation optional; finalize the Slack decision (Nadim + Sue to follow up)
8. **Track victories and progress** in a shared, visible space (e.g., Slack channel or Notion)
9. **Weekly async check-ins** -- each circle lead posts a brief update in the comms channel covering what they've done, what's next, and any blockers

### Partnerships, Funding & Activation
10. **Finalize MOUs and partnership agreements** between ecosystem entities to clarify roles, authority, and how the collective narrative is represented by each org when speaking to funders
11. **Clarify funding flow mechanics** -- how does capital move through the trust, to contracted orgs (e.g., BE, Kinship Earth), and into bioregional activations? Establish clear rules so that when money arrives, distribution is already agreed upon (per Sue's caution about friction when funding lands without pre-agreed structures)
12. **Define bioregional activation protocol components** -- JC's near-term homework: what are the protocol components required for a community activation, what exists already, and what needs to be built?
13. **Explore learning partnerships** -- engage allies like Region Commons for input on commons governance in practice; consider training in agile/self-management when budget allows
14. **Website and public-facing narrative** -- JC to finalize Theory of Change integration into the website so the ecosystem has a shareable, credible front door for partners and funders

## Alternative: Project Manager Path

If the team prefers a single coordinator over a council:

- Name a Project Manager with formal recognition and clear mandate
- Responsibilities: scheduling, agenda-setting, follow-ups, timeline tracking, proposal synthesis
- Uses advice method (not command-and-control)
- Must be compensated -- this is substantial, ongoing work
