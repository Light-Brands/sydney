## **4\. What Is Investable vs Donor-Funded**

### **Donor-Funded (Commons & Care)**

Donor capital supports:

* Governance & administration

* Legal & fiscal containers

* Coordination & communications

* Flow Funds

* Community engagement & assemblies

* Storytelling & learning

Why: These functions hold trust, equity, and long-term impact.

---

### **Investable (Durable Infrastructure)**

Investor capital may support:

* Bioregional Intelligence Dashboard

* Learning infrastructure & certification pathways

* Toolkits, playbooks, and service platforms

* Interoperable tech systems

Why: These assets create long-term utility and potential returns.

Investment  within the Planetary Party Protocol is always governed by the commons.

Capital is deployed into activities that generate revenue and surplus, and returns are tied to those cash flows \- not to ownership or control.

Investment does not confer equity, voting rights, or decision-making power. Governance remains with the people and communities directly involved in and impacted by the work.

All investment opportunities are structured through Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs) that sit within a commons-aligned trust framework, ensuring that capital serves the long-term health of the ecosystem rather than extracting value from it.

The commons aligned trust framework refers to a legal and governance container where assets, IP, land, revenue rights, or operating vehicles are:

* Held in trust for a shared purpose (the commons)  
* Governed by clear stewardship obligations, not private ownership  
* Protected from capture, sale, or control by capital  
* Oriented toward long-term regenerative benefit, not short-term extraction

The trust exists to protect the mission, the community, and the ecosystem \- not to maximize private gain.

### **When speaking with** 

### **donors**

* Emphasize equity, care, and long-term impact

* Do not promise financial return

### **When speaking with** 

### **investors**

* Lead with platforms, tools, and infrastructure

* Emphasize governance as risk reduction

* Be clear about ROI being long-term and impact-aligned

### **mixed audiences**

* Name that different capital plays different roles

* Emphasize sequencing and coherence

* Avoid blurring donation and investment expectations
