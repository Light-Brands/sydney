# The Bioregional Activation Series

**Co-creating the relational, governance, and coordination foundations needed to resource thriving bioregions**

---

## What Is This?

The Bioregional Activation Series is a co-created gathering process designed to prepare bioregions for deeper coordination, shared resourcing, and self-determination. It is not a conference or a program to be delivered -- it is a living process of listening, mapping, and weaving that meets each bioregion where it is and builds from what already exists.

The series emerges from the convergence of bioregional organizing, regenerative finance, land-based community building, governance education, and culture-making. Co-stewarded by Syd Harvey Griffith and Susanna Choe, it is rooted in a simple premise: the people and projects needed for bioregional thriving are already present -- what's missing is the relational infrastructure to see each other, trust each other, and coordinate together.

The core question: **Who is already doing this work, what do they need, and how do we build the foundations to resource a thriving bioregion together?**

## Why This Matters

Across bioregions worldwide, regenerative practitioners, land stewards, organizers, technologists, funders, and culture bearers are arriving at the same conclusions independently. They are building overlapping infrastructure without visibility into each other's efforts. Communities carry deep knowledge of their places but lack the coordination tools, governance literacy, and financial mechanisms to act on that knowledge at the bioregional scale.

The Bioregional Activation Series addresses this gap -- not by imposing a centralized structure, but by creating the conditions for shared action to emerge organically from trust, relationship, and place-based intelligence.

Each activation is shaped by the unique cultural, ecological, and political context of its bioregion. The pillars remain consistent; the expression is always local.

## What It Aims to Produce

Each bioregional activation is oriented toward tangible outcomes that emerge through relationship rather than top-down design:

1. **A Bioregional Flow Fund** -- a trust-based, participatory funding mechanism rooted in relationship and local stewardship, where communities closest to the work decide where capital flows.

2. **A Bioregional Currency / Token** -- a practical coordination and exchange tool with governance layers explored when the bioregion is ready, reducing dependency on extractive financial systems.

3. **Development and fundraising capacity for land-based hubs** -- strengthening the farms, community centers, bioregional finance facilities, and project sites already doing the work.

4. **Commitment pooling across the bioregion** -- coordinating time, skills, relationships, land access, and funding to strengthen collaboration and mutual aid.

5. **A bioregional festival** -- a leave-a-positive-trace celebration co-created through the Planetary Party Protocol, where every gathering leaves lasting regenerative infrastructure.

The specific outcomes will vary by bioregion. In some places, the Flow Fund is the most urgent need. In others, it may be mapping, governance capacity, or simply building enough trust for people to begin collaborating across historically siloed efforts.

## The Pillars

The series is organized around six interwoven pillars that hold across every bioregion:

**Relationship-Building & Mapping** is the foundation. Before building anything, the series asks: who is already stewarding land, capital, governance, culture, or exchange? Where are the natural synergies? This pillar focuses on coherence, not speed -- ensuring that whatever structures emerge are rooted in trust and shared understanding.

**Governance Education** is woven throughout as living infrastructure -- shared language around decision-making, exposure to participatory and commons-based governance models, and practical support for co-creating across difference without fragmentation.

**Tools, Tech & Frameworks** surfaces the technologies and coordination platforms already supporting the movement -- mapping tools, participatory governance frameworks, regenerative finance infrastructure, storytelling platforms -- and invites the builders into the room so the bioregion can see what's available, what's interoperable, and what's missing.

**Healing & Conflict Transformation** is made available responsively, not centered. Every community carries friction and unresolved tensions that can block collaboration. Skilled facilitators hold space for acknowledgment and integration without derailing the broader work.

**Honoring Place** grounds each activation in the governance wisdom, Indigenous traditions, and living ecologies of the specific bioregion. In the Northeast, this means centering the Haudenosaunee Great Law of Peace. In Mexico, it means honoring Mesoamerican traditions of land stewardship and communal governance. The form changes; the commitment to place remains.

**Culture, Arts & Creative Expression** invites music, visual art, storytelling, and ceremony as forms of cultural infrastructure -- supporting shared identity, celebration, and imaginative vision alongside the structural work.

## How It Unfolds

Each activation moves through three phases:

**Online preparation sessions** introduce the purpose, orient participants to shared language and bioregional context, and invite advance mapping so a living picture of the field already exists when people gather in person.

**In-person gatherings at anchor sites** create space for embodied relationship-building, sensemaking, governance practice, storytelling, and design labs -- translating mapped insights into lived connection and next steps.

**Distributed hub gatherings** extend the work to partner-hosted activations at farms, community centers, and land-based hubs across the bioregion -- locally relevant expressions connected back to shared learning and coordination.

## Current Activations

Kinship Earth is currently engaged in and resourcing bioregional activations in two regions:

**Northeast Turtle Island -- launching Spring 2026.** Co-stewarded with Susanna Choe and the Bioregional Embassy, with anchor sites including Hudson Harmonic. The NE activation draws on Permatours' existing network of 40+ land-based project sites, contributions from technology partners (Spatial Network, Catalist, Social Roots, Hylo, Bloom Network), and land-based communities including True North, Hudson Harmonic, and Wally Farms. The series honors the Haudenosaunee Great Law of Peace as the US approaches its 250th anniversary -- a moment to reconnect bioregional governance with the Indigenous traditions of collective stewardship that originated in this land.

**Mexico -- launching Summer 2026.** Building on relationships Kinship Earth and the Planetary Party have been cultivating since late 2025 in the Cloud Forest bioregion (Veracruz) and Mexico City. This activation will be shaped by the communities, practitioners, and governance traditions already present -- centering Mesoamerican land stewardship, communal governance, and the unique ecological and cultural context of the bioregions involved.

Additional bioregions where Kinship Earth has active Flow Funder relationships -- the Ozarks, Cascadia, Greater Tkaronto, Colombia, Jamaica, Guatemala -- represent future possibilities for activation as readiness and relationships deepen.

## How the Series Lives Across Syd's Ecosystem

The Bioregional Activation Series is not a standalone project -- it is the convergence point for all of Syd's initiatives, and it is relevant to every one of them.

**Kinship Earth** provides the financial backbone and the Flow Funding methodology each activation is preparing bioregions to adopt. The series is a direct expression of Kinship Earth's mission: helping communities build the readiness to launch their own trust-based Flow Funds.

**Planetary Party Protocol** provides the coordination architecture -- its five-phase rhythm (Sense, See, Flow, Celebrate, Regenerate), guild structure, and bioregional festival model give each activation a shared scaffolding without centralizing control.

**Permatours** is the ground-level network that makes the NE activation real -- 40+ land-based project sites, mutual aid infrastructure, and existing community relationships that the series builds upon and accelerates.

**Unified Sanctuaries** represents what the series is building toward: place-based regenerative community rooted in land. Its open-source governance and economic models become resources for every hub the series surfaces.

**Live with Syd** sustains the series between gatherings through weekly co-creative sessions, storytelling, and community cultivation -- the ongoing relational container and content pipeline that feeds fundraising and coherence across the effort.

**Women of the Turning** shapes how the series convenes -- bringing feminine leadership, somatic wisdom, and ceremonial depth into the governance, healing, and relational practices at the heart of every activation.

The series is where the ecosystem meets the ground. Each initiative contributes something the others cannot, and each is strengthened by what the activations produce: new relationships, new maps, new governance capacity, and new bioregional infrastructure that serves communities far beyond Syd's direct orbit.
