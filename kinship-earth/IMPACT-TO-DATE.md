# Kinship Earth: Impact to Date

**Resourcing Systemic Change & Healing Through Trust-Based Flow Funding**

---

## Who We Are

Kinship Earth is a private foundation practicing **Flow Funding** -- a trust-based, participatory grantmaking model that moves unrestricted resources directly to grassroots leaders and bioregional organizers. No grant applications. Minimal reporting. Grounded in the principle that **the people closest to the problem are closest to the solution.**

## Flow Funding Cohorts & Thematic Focus Areas

### Cohort 1 -- October 2024

*Kinship Earth's inaugural cohort launched the Flow Fund and inaugurated its Community of Practice.*

**Thematic focus areas:**

- **Youth & Education** -- Investing in mentorship and early childhood development for underrepresented communities, including young Black girls in dance and Indigenous Head Start programs (Oklahoma)
- **Community Strengthening & Mentorship** -- Building networks of support for fathers, families, and immigrant and refugee mothers through peer mentorship and acts of care (Oklahoma, nationwide)
- **Food Sovereignty & Land-Based Regeneration** -- Growing 5,000+ lbs of donated food on Cape Cod; restoring food sovereignty across 12 villages in Nigeria; supporting permaculture food forests in Ecuador; and securing winter heating and food delivery infrastructure on Pine Ridge Reservation (Massachusetts, Nigeria, Ecuador, South Dakota)
- **Environmental Defense & Advocacy** -- Enabling a critical legal victory protecting Lake Superior from toxic mining waste, including stopping dam construction through emergency litigation (Minnesota)
- **Indigenous Sovereignty** -- Supporting treaty rights, land rematriation, and self-determination for Indigenous communities
- **Ecosystem Restoration** -- Piloting 8 landscape hubs across the Cascadia Bioregion coordinating regeneration at scale; supporting 10 women restoring coastal forest ecosystems across five East African countries

### Cohort 2 -- February 2025

*Kinship Earth's second cohort deepened its bioregional focus, resourcing the Greater Tkaronto Bioregion.*

**Thematic focus areas:**

- **Bioregional Organizing** -- Resourcing grassroots leaders and community organizers in the Greater Tkaronto Bioregion (southern Ontario, Canada)
- **Community Resilience** -- Strengthening local networks of collaboration, mutual support, and regenerative action
- **Trust-Based Philanthropy in Practice** -- Expanding the model of entrusting community leaders to direct capital where it is most needed

### Cohort 3 -- January 2026

*Kinship Earth's third cohort marks its most geographically expansive deployment, resourcing bioregions across the Americas.*

**Thematic focus areas:**

- **International Bioregional Expansion** -- Deploying $70,000+ to grassroots leaders and organizing groups in Colombia, Mexico, Jamaica, and the Northeast of Turtle Island
- **Latin American & Caribbean Solidarity** -- Resourcing community-led regeneration, including work in Montego Bay (Jamaica) and the Cloud Forest bioregion (Mexico)
- **Northeast Turtle Island Activation** -- Supporting bioregional organizing, community engagement, and regenerative initiatives across the U.S. Northeast
- **Cross-Bioregional Learning** -- Weaving relationships and shared learning across diverse geographies and cultures

## Thematic Impact Across All Cohorts

Across three cohorts, Kinship Earth's Flow Funding has resourced work spanning six interconnected areas of impact:

1. **Regeneration of Natural Systems** -- Reforestation, soil restoration, permaculture, and landscape-scale regeneration
2. **Indigenous Rights & Land Rematriation** -- Upholding sovereignty, treaty rights, and self-determination
3. **Food Sovereignty & Security** -- Community gardens, food forests, agricultural programs, and winter food preservation
4. **Social Justice & Economic Equity** -- Youth mentorship, fatherhood development, refugee support, and community care
5. **Environmental Defense** -- Legal advocacy protecting watersheds, sacred waters, and ecosystems from extractive industry
6. **Ecosystem & Human Health** -- Holistic wellbeing, healing practices, and community resilience

## What Makes This Work Different

> *"Flow Funding isn't just about mitigating harm; it's also about fueling joy, desire, and the audacity to create... even in hard times."*
> -- Je Amaechi, Flow Funder

- **Trust over compliance** -- Unrestricted grants with lightweight, story-based accountability
- **Community-led decisions** -- Flow Funders decide how capital moves, not the foundation
- **Self-perpetuating network** -- Each cohort of Flow Funders recommends the next
- **Bioregional lens** -- Organized around natural ecosystems and culture, not political boundaries
- **Relational accountability** -- A Community of Practice brings Flow Funders, team, and donors into shared learning
