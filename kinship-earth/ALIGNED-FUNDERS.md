# Research: Funders Aligned with Kinship Earth's Grantmaking Focus Areas

> **Date:** March 3, 2026
>
> **Purpose:** Identify organizations, grantors, DAFs, corporate foundations, institutional funders, and others who publicly practice one or more of the following — and who accept inquiries, grant applications, LOIs, or relationship-based outreach.
>
> **Focus Areas Researched:**
> - Trust-based participatory grantmaking
> - Community-directed unrestricted grantmaking
> - Place-based regenerative grantmaking with measurable community outcomes
> - Participatory regrantmaking to community-based organizations
> - Bioregional, place-based grantmaking at the intersection of ecological restoration and community resilience
> - Participatory, trust-based grantmaking centering community self-determination / communities control resource allocation / decenters funder power

## Funders with Open Applications / LOIs

### 1. Regenerative Agriculture Foundation (RAF)

- **Website:** <https://regenerativeagriculturefoundation.org/our-grants/>
- **Focus areas matched:** Trust-based participatory grantmaking; place-based regenerative grantmaking; bioregional ecological restoration + community resilience

### 2. Patagonia Environmental Grants

- **Website:** <https://www.patagonia.com/how-we-fund/>
- **Focus areas matched:** Community-directed grantmaking; bioregional ecological restoration + community resilience
- **Details:**
  - Supports grassroots activist organizations working on root causes of the environmental crisis
  - Funds habitat restoration, community-led conservation, and environmental resilience
  - Prioritizes small organizations (budgets under $1M)
  - Interested in organizations with provocative, direct-action agendas and commitment to long-term change
- **Grant size:** $5,000–$20,000
- **Application:** Open, rolling deadlines (typically May 31, Sept 30, Jan 31)
  - Corporate Grants: <https://www.patagonia.com/how-we-fund/corporate-grant/>
  - International Grants: <https://www.patagonia.com/how-we-fund/international-grant/>
- **Eligibility:** 501(c)(3) nonprofits or fiscally sponsored groups with budgets under $1M

### 3. Ronald W. Naito MD Foundation

- **Website:** <https://www.rwnfoundation.org/grants/how-to-apply/>
- **Focus areas matched:** Trust-based unrestricted grantmaking; community self-determination; ecological restoration + community resilience
- **Details:**
  - All grants are unrestricted and trust-based — respects the expertise of nonprofit partners

## Invitation-Only / Relationship-Based (Worth Cultivating)

### 19. Thousand Currents + CLIMA Fund

- **Website:** <https://thousandcurrents.org/our-approach/>
- **Focus areas matched:** Trust-based unrestricted grantmaking; community self-determination; ecological resilience; decentering funder power
- **Details:**
  - Sustained, unrestricted grants to grassroots partners; expressly decenters funder power and acknowledges its own positional privilege
  - Theory of change: supporting self-determination of communities, building organizational resilience, facilitating alliance building, informing social justice grantmaking
  - CLIMA Fund (with Grassroots International, Global Greengrants Fund, Urgent Action Fund) funds grassroots climate resilience across Africa, Americas, Asia, Middle East, Caribbean, Pacific Islands
  - $60M+ invested in 650+ groups across 85 countries
  - Partners work on regenerative agriculture, traditional seed systems, communal land/forest stewardship, watershed protection
- **Access:** Does not accept unsolicited applications — proactively identifies partners. Build relationships through their network
- **Geography:** Africa, Asia and the Pacific, Latin America (Global South focus)

### 20. Kataly Foundation — Restorative Economies Fund

- **Website:** <https://www.katalyfoundation.org/program/restorative-economies-fund/>
- **Focus areas matched:** Community self-determination; participatory; regenerative; trust-based
- **Details:**
  - Integrated capital fund combining grants with non-extractive investments (loans, loan guarantees, lines of credit)
  - Supports community-owned and governed projects led by people of color
  - Builds collective political, economic, and cultural power
  - Also launched 2025 Solidarity and Resilience Fund ($3.45M to 67 organizations)
  - Founded by Regan Pritzker; $445M initial endowment; planned spend-down within a decade
- **Grant size:** $10K–$500K+
- **Access:** Relationship-driven; contact info@katalyfoundation.org
