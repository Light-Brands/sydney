**Organizational Overview**

Kinship Earth is transforming how catalytic philanthropic capital reaches grassroots communities and ecosystems. Our mission is to catalyze planetary regeneration by moving unrestricted resources directly to trusted community leaders and bioregional organizing groups through Flow Funding \- a participatory, trust-based grantmaking model that replaces application-driven philanthropy with invitation-driven, community-controlled capital deployment.

Our vision is a world where catalytic capital reliably flows to the communities closest to impact \- governed by trust and relational accountability rather than the compliance barriers that structurally exclude them. For example, Indigenous peoples steward 80% of global biodiversity yet receive less than 1% of climate finance. Flow Funding closes this gap.

We operate across 4 functions: (1) deploying unrestricted grants to trusted changemakers across bioregions; (2) educating capital holders on trust-based catalytic deployment; (3) building field-level infrastructure \- including open-access playbooks, legal templates, and governance frameworks so communities worldwide can launch their own Flow Funds; and (4) activating bioregions through co-created gatherings that build the relational, governance, and coordination foundations for launching catalytic capital vehicles.

Since mid-2024, we have raised $800K+ and deployed $360K+ to 23 Flow Funders across 12+ bioregions through three cohorts. Our work spans the Caribbean, Latin America, East Africa, and multiple bioregions across Turtle Island (U.S.) \- resourcing food sovereignty, ecosystem restoration, Indigenous rights/land rematriation, environmental defense, and community resilience.

Our Communities of Practice bring Flow Funders, team, and donors into shared learning, creating a feedback loop that strengthens the model with each cycle. Each cohort recommends the next, building a self-perpetuating, decentralized network of catalytic capital deployment.
