**Kinship Earth’s Expression of Interest | C3 2026 Grantmaking | Grant Request: $150,000**

**From Field-Tested to Field-Ready: Playbooks for Deploying and Expanding Bioregional Catalytic Capital**

**Kinship Earth** is a foundation practicing Flow Funding, a trust-based, participatory grantmaking model that moves unrestricted capital directly to grassroots leaders and bioregional organizing groups. Our successful model has been field-tested across 12 bioregions with $360K deployed to 23 Flow Funders across 3 cohorts since late 2024\. This grant will enable us to finalize, field-test, and publish two free, open-access playbooks drawn from this operational experience: a Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook (a modular toolkit for communities to launch and govern bioregional Flow Funds); and a Bioregional Flow Fueling Playbook (an onboarding / deployment guide for funders seeking to provide catalytic capital). These resources will give communities and capital providers the infrastructure to start and fund trust-based grantmaking vehicles.

**Catalytic philanthropic capital is underleveraged due to the following barriers:**

**1\. No replicable deployment infrastructure:** Trust-based grantmaking vehicles are often built from scratch \- duplicating legal structuring, governance, and operations. This inflates costs, extends timelines, and limits the number of vehicles that reach deployment.

**2\. Untapped capital providers on the sidelines**: Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs) hold $230B+ with no payout requirement. Funders want to resource grassroots regeneration but lack actionable on-ramps. Without field-tested models, willing capital sits idle.

**3\. Grassroots communities excluded**: Compliance-heavy grantmaking locks out effective actors (e.g., indigenous peoples protect 80% of biodiversity yet receive \<1% of climate finance). The current system channels capital toward grant-writing capacity, not proximity to impact.

**Proposed Solution:** Kinship Earth will finalize, field-test, and publish the following two playbooks which address the barriers in catalytic capital supply and deployment:

**1\. A Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook:** This playbook will provide modular tools for communities to launch and govern bioregional Flow Funds, including legal templates that can be adapted across jurisdictions, governance frameworks, and operational guides that reduce time-to-launch for trust-based vehicles.

**2\. A Bioregional Flow Fueling Playbook**: This playbook will offer onboarding pathways, decision frameworks, and deployment guides for funders. It will include onboarding tools and decision frameworks to convert inactive capital holders into active catalytic capital providers resulting in open-access resources that channel capital into deployable community-level deals and expand the pool of providers.                                                                                                       

**Why It’s Needed:** Trust-based philanthropy is gaining traction, but the field lacks practical vehicles organized around bioregions, defined by watersheds, ecosystems, and cultures. The bioregional organizers in our cohorts have expressed that the guidance we have provided has been essential to building out their own Flow Funds and that a structured playbook would accelerate their progress.

**Activities:**
