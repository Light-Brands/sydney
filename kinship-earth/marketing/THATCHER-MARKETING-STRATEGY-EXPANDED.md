## 1. Prerequisites Before Launching Google Ads Grant

Before spending $10K/month driving traffic, we need conversion points, engagement pathways, and backend infrastructure ready. Otherwise we're paying to bring people to a house with no doors.

### Website Readiness (kinshipearth.org)

- **Google Ads Grant compliance requirements** -- Google is stringent about site quality before approving the grant. Thatcher will audit the Wix site and flag what needs to change before application.
- **Clear calls to action on every page** -- Every landing page needs at least one obvious next step: subscribe, donate, read a story, join a community, volunteer, share.
- **Dedicated landing pages per campaign type** -- Don't send all ad traffic to the homepage. Build specific pages for:
  - Newsletter signup (primary conversion goal)
  - Donate (with tiered options, see Section 4)
  - Flow Funder stories / impact page (the "why" page)
  - Volunteer signup
  - Bioregional Activation Series overview (for regional campaigns)
  - "What is Flow Funding?" explainer (for people searching for trust-based philanthropy, community-directed giving, etc.)
- **Mobile optimization** -- Most ad traffic will land on mobile. Every landing page and donation form must work smoothly on phones.
- **SSL, site speed, accessibility** -- Google Ads Grant requirements; also just good practice.

### Email / Newsletter Infrastructure

- **Choose and configure email platform** -- Options discussed: HubSpot (already in use for Permatours), Beehive (strong for integration marketing and cross-newsletter promotion). Decision needed on whether to use HubSpot for Kinship or set up a separate Beehive instance.
- **Welcome sequence built** -- When someone subscribes via a Google Ad, they should immediately receive:
  1. Welcome email (who we are, what flow funding is, a compelling story)
  2. Follow-up 2--3 days later (deeper story, invite to follow on social / join WhatsApp)
  3. First ask or engagement prompt (small donation, share with a friend, volunteer interest form)
- **Consistent sending cadence established** -- Thatcher recommends 2x/week if possible. Even 1x/week is fine to start, but the cadence needs to be committed to *before* traffic starts flowing. An email list that doesn't hear from you goes cold fast.
- **Integration marketing setup** -- After someone joins the Kinship Earth newsletter, auto-suggest related newsletters (e.g., Syd's personal "Live with Syd" newsletter, Planetary Party updates, Permatours). This is where Beehive excels.

### CRM & Backend (HubSpot + Notion + Slack)

- **HubSpot** -- Use as the CRM for tracking donors, volunteers, and newsletter subscribers. Set up:
  - Contact properties for donor type (small donor, major donor prospect, volunteer, Flow Fund Advisor, etc.)
  - Pipeline for donor journey stages (new subscriber > engaged reader > small donor > recurring donor > major donor prospect)
  - Automated tagging based on how someone entered the system (which Google Ad campaign, which landing page, which form)
  - Email sequences / workflows for the welcome series and donor nurture
- **Notion** -- Continue using for team project management, content calendars, and Live with Syd planning. Create a shared workspace or page for Thatcher collaboration:
  - Campaign tracker (ad copy drafts, status, performance)
  - Content calendar for newsletter topics
  - Meeting notes and action items
- **Slack** -- Use for real-time coordination between Syd, Nadim, and Thatcher. Consider a dedicated channel (e.g., #kinship-marketing) for campaign updates, draft reviews, and quick decisions.

### Donation Infrastructure

- **Online donation flow** -- Ensure the donate page is frictionless: minimal fields, multiple payment options, mobile-friendly. If using Wix's built-in donations, test the full flow. Consider whether a dedicated tool (e.g., Donorbox, Every.org, or HubSpot's payment links) would convert better.
- **Recurring donation option** -- Critical for small-dollar strategy. Make monthly giving easy and prominent (see Section 4).
- **Donation tiers with impact anchors** -- Give people a reason to pick a specific amount by tying it to real outcomes:
  - $10/month = "Sustains our newsletter and community engagement"
  - $25/month = "Supports a Flow Funder's story being told and shared"
  - $50/month = "Contributes to seeding a new bioregional Flow Fund"
  - $100/month = "Directly resources grassroots leaders through flow funding"
- **Tax receipt automation** -- Donors should get an immediate, professional receipt. Set this up before launching any campaigns.

### Tracking & Analytics

- **Google Analytics 4** -- Must be installed and configured on the Wix site (required for Google Ads Grant).
- **Conversion tracking** -- Set up conversion events for: newsletter signup, donation completed, volunteer form submitted, WhatsApp community join.
- **UTM parameters** -- Every Google Ad campaign should use UTM tags so we can trace which campaigns drive which actions.

## 2. Google Ads Grant -- Setup & Ongoing Management

### How It Works
- $10,000/month in Google Search ad credits for eligible 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- Ads appear in Google Search results when people search relevant terms
- Budget does NOT roll over day to day -- must spend ~$329/day to use the full grant
- Requires active management to maintain click-through rates and spend levels

### Campaign Types (Not Just Fundraising)

Thatcher emphasized that the most effective approach is NOT to lead with "give us money." Instead, build the relationship first:

1. **Newsletter signup campaigns** (primary) -- "Discover how trust-based funding is transforming communities" > lands on newsletter signup page
2. **Story / awareness campaigns** -- "See what happens when communities decide where money flows" > lands on Flow Funder stories page
3. **Volunteer recruitment** -- "Join a global movement of people resourcing bioregional regeneration" > lands on volunteer interest form
4. **Community join campaigns** -- Drive traffic directly to WhatsApp or Facebook community (Thatcher suggested this as an option)
5. **Donation campaigns** (secondary, once list is warm) -- "Your $25 seeds a bioregional Flow Fund" > lands on donate page
6. **Bioregion-specific campaigns** -- Target search terms relevant to specific bioregions we're active in (see Section 5)

### Key Search Terms to Target
- Trust-based philanthropy / participatory grantmaking
- Community-directed giving / unrestricted grants
- Bioregional organizing / regenerative communities
- Flow funding / flow funds
- How to support Indigenous communities / environmental justice giving
- Alternatives to traditional philanthropy
- Volunteer for environmental / regenerative work

### Permatours Google Ads Grant
- Permatours already has an approved Google Ads Grant but it may be dormant/unused
- Thatcher should audit the status before it gets canceled
- Could be reactivated and managed alongside Kinship Earth's grant

## 4. Avenues for Smaller Donations ($1--$100+)

This is an area Kinship Earth hasn't focused on yet. The big-donor strategy takes time and deep relationship building. Meanwhile, a grassroots fundraising engine can provide baseline revenue, build community ownership, and create a pipeline of future major donors.

### Recurring Monthly Giving Program

- **Name it** -- Give the program an identity people can belong to (e.g., "Flow Circle," "Kinship Keepers," "Seeders"). People donate to belong, not just to give.
- **Low barrier entry** -- Start at $5/month. The goal is volume and community, not large individual amounts.
- **Tiered recognition** -- Monthly donors get:
  - Exclusive newsletter content or early access to stories
  - Name listed on a "community of supporters" page (opt-in)
  - Quarterly update from Syd specifically for sustaining donors
  - Invitation to a quarterly video call / AMA with Syd or a Flow Funder
- **Promote through the newsletter** -- Once the email list is built via Google Ads, recurring giving becomes a regular soft ask woven into story-based content.

### One-Time Donation Campaigns

- **Matching campaigns** -- Line up agreements with larger donors to match small donations during specific drives. "Every dollar you give this week is doubled by our matching fund." These are proven high-converters.
- **Story-driven appeals** -- Feature a specific Flow Funder story and tie the ask directly to it: "This is what $1,000 in seeds became. Imagine what your $25 could grow."
- **Seasonal / milestone campaigns** -- End of year giving, anniversary of first cohort, launch of a new bioregional activation, reaching X number of bioregions served.
- **Birthday and celebration fundraisers** -- Encourage supporters to create Facebook/Instagram fundraisers for Kinship Earth on their birthdays. Provide a toolkit (suggested copy, images, links) to make it effortless.

### Merchandise

- **For-profit / nonprofit partnership model** -- A for-profit entity (or aligned partner) donates or sells product to Kinship Earth, which offers it as a gift with donation or sells it with proceeds benefiting the mission.
- **Potential merch ideas:**
  - Kinship Earth branded items (hats, shirts, stickers, tote bags)
  - Collaborations with Flow Funders or bioregional artisans (telling a story through the product)
  - Hemp-based products (natural alignment with Thatcher's expertise and network -- Eat More Hemp, hemp bioplastics)
- **Use Google Ads grant traffic** to build the newsletter, then promote merch through the newsletter (not directly through ads, since the grant is for nonprofit purposes).

### Crowdfunding Campaigns

- **Campaign-specific crowdfunding** for tangible goals: "Help us launch the Northeast Bioregional Flow Fund" or "Resource 5 new Flow Funders in Cohort 4"
- Platforms: GoFundMe Charity, Every.org (designed for nonprofits), or direct on-site
- Share through newsletter, social media, WhatsApp communities

### Social Media Micro-Donations

- **Chatbot engagement** (Thatcher's recommendation) -- When people comment on Instagram/Facebook posts (e.g., "Comment FLOW to learn how you can help"), an automated message sends them a link to donate, subscribe, or join the community.
- **Instagram / Facebook donation stickers** in stories
- **TikTok-style short-form content** featuring Flow Funder stories, paired with donation links

### Peer-to-Peer Fundraising

- Empower existing supporters to fundraise on Kinship Earth's behalf
- Provide a simple toolkit: template social posts, one-pagers, suggested messaging
- Especially powerful during bioregional activations -- attendees can fundraise within their own networks

### Event-Based Giving

- **Live with Syd** sessions could include a gentle donation prompt
- **Bioregional Activation Series** events -- in-person or hybrid -- with a donation component
- **Partner events** (see Section 5) where Kinship Earth has a presence and a QR code to donate

## 5. Bioregional & Local Partnerships

This is where Kinship Earth's unique positioning creates real leverage. Instead of generic national fundraising, we can partner with organizations, businesses, and communities in the specific bioregions where we're active -- making the case hyper-local and tangible.

### Partnership Types

#### Bioregional Organizing Partners
- **Regenerate Cascadia** (already a Flow Funder) -- co-fundraise for the Cascadia bioregional Flow Fund. They have their own community; we have the infrastructure and story. Joint campaigns multiply reach.
- **Northeast bioregional network** -- As the NE Bioregional Activation Series launches (Spring 2026), identify local organizations, land projects, and community groups who would co-promote and co-fundraise.
- **Colombia, Jamaica, Mexico Cloud Forest** -- Each Cohort 3 bioregion has local partners who could amplify campaigns within their networks.
- **Greater Tkaronto** -- Cohort 2 partners in southern Ontario.
- **Turtle Island Congress** -- Continental-scale bioregional convening network; potential for cross-promotion and shared fundraising.

#### Aligned Nonprofits & Networks
- **Bloom Network, Hylo, Social Roots, Catalist, Spatial Network** -- Technology partners already mentioned in the Bioregional Activation Series. Cross-promote through their platforms and communities.
- **Bioregional Embassy** (co-stewarding NE Activation with Susanna Choe) -- joint visibility and fundraising.
- **Trust-based philanthropy networks** -- Connect with organizations in the trust-based philanthropy space (e.g., Trust-Based Philanthropy Project, NCRP, Grassroots Grantmakers) for visibility and credibility.

#### Local Businesses & Social Enterprises
- **Regenerative farms, food co-ops, land-based hubs** in active bioregions -- Partner on events, offer merch, cross-promote. These businesses have local customer bases who care about the same things.
- **Hemp companies and sustainable brands** -- Thatcher's network includes hemp food, bioplastics, and sustainable product companies. Natural co-marketing opportunities (e.g., "X% of proceeds supports Kinship Earth's flow funding work").
- **Breweries, coffee roasters, local shops** in bioregions we're active in -- "Round up your purchase for Kinship Earth" or co-branded products.

#### Music, Arts & Festival Partnerships
- **Permatours network** -- 40+ land-based project sites and a community of thousands. Even if Permatours transitions to a mutual aid model, the community is a natural audience.
- **Building Man and similar festivals** -- Presence at aligned festivals with merch, QR codes, speaking slots, and community engagement.
- **Local New England bands and artists** -- Partner with musicians who align with the mission. They promote Kinship Earth to their audience; Kinship Earth features them in content. Works especially well for Permatours and the NE Bioregional Activation.
- **Planetary Party events** -- As the Planetary Party Protocol develops bioregional festivals, each one is a fundraising and community-building moment for Kinship Earth.

#### High-Net-Worth Individual & Foundation Partnerships
- **Landbreak (Danish billionaire's rewilding initiative)** -- Thatcher flagged this. Potential outreach for the bioregional rewilding / land acquisition angle.
- **Family offices, DAFs, foundations on the aligned funders list** -- Thatcher can help craft outreach subject lines and email sequences to get these donors to open and engage.
- **Matching fund agreements** -- Secure commitments from larger donors to match grassroots campaigns, making small-dollar drives dramatically more effective.

### How Google Ads Support Bioregional Partnerships

- **Geo-targeted campaigns** -- Run Google Ads targeting people in specific bioregions searching for local regenerative, environmental, or community terms.
  - Example: Someone in Vermont searching "support local farms Vermont" sees an ad about how Kinship Earth resources community-led food sovereignty in the Northeast.
  - Example: Someone in the Pacific Northwest searching "bioregional organizing Cascadia" finds Regenerate Cascadia's story through a Kinship Earth ad.
- **Partner-specific landing pages** -- Create co-branded or partner-featured landing pages that tell the local story and invite local engagement (subscribe, donate, volunteer, attend activation event).
- **Drive traffic to bioregional community hubs** -- Some ad spend could funnel people directly into bioregion-specific WhatsApp groups or Facebook communities, building local organizing capacity alongside the national list.

## 6. Content Collection & Story Readiness

**Syd's instinct is right: collect more stories before kicking off Google Ads.** The ads will bring people to the door -- we need rich, compelling content waiting for them when they arrive.

### What We Already Have (Abundance of Content)

- **23+ Flow Funder stories** across 3 cohorts and 12+ bioregions (written up in the Flow Funder Stories of Impact document and the Kinship Flow Funder Stories PDF)
- **Impact data** -- $800K+ raised, $360K+ deployed, 12+ bioregions, 3 cohorts
- **Bioregional Activation Series** documentation and proposals
- **Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook** (publishing end of Q1 2026)
- **Executive Summary and pitch materials**
- **Guatemala immersion** end-of-year newsletter and content
- **Cohort 1 impact report**
- **Large content archive** on the Kinship Earth file server (Nadim flagged this -- lots of repurposable content)

### What We Need More Of

- **Short-form video content** -- 60-90 second clips of Flow Funders sharing their story in their own words. This is the most powerful content for social media, newsletters, and landing pages. Prioritize collecting these from willing Flow Funders across all 3 cohorts.
- **Photo documentation** -- High-quality images from bioregional projects, activations, and Flow Funders in action. Even phone photos that are authentic and compelling.
- **Bioregion-specific content packages** -- For each active bioregion, we need:
  - 1 anchor story (long-form, detailed, with quotes and outcomes)
  - 2--3 shorter story snippets for newsletters and social media
  - At least 3--5 images or video clips
  - A 1-paragraph "what we're doing in [bioregion]" summary for landing pages
- **"What is Flow Funding?" explainer content** -- Short, accessible content for people discovering us through Google Ads who have never heard of flow funding. Could be a short video, an infographic, or a well-written page.
- **Live with Syd content** -- Once Syd starts going live, each session produces content that can be repurposed into newsletter segments, social clips, and landing page material. This is a content engine.
- **Donor testimonials** -- Why do our donors give? What drew them to trust-based philanthropy? These are powerful for converting newsletter subscribers into donors.

### Content Collection Action Plan

1. **Audit existing content** -- Nadim and Syd inventory what's on the file server and categorize by bioregion, theme, and format (text, photo, video).
2. **Identify gaps** -- Which bioregions have thin content? Which stories are told in long-form but not yet adapted for short-form / social / newsletter?
3. **Reach out to Flow Funders** -- Send a simple request to willing Flow Funders: "Would you record a 60-second video about what flow funding made possible for your community?" Provide a prompt template.
4. **Repurpose existing long-form stories** -- Break each Flow Funder story into 3--5 newsletter-sized snippets and social media posts. Schedule these across the content calendar.
5. **Build bioregion landing pages** -- Using collected content, create at least one landing page per active bioregion before launching geo-targeted Google Ads.
6. **Launch Live with Syd** -- This creates a recurring source of fresh content and builds Syd's thought leadership, which feeds back into donor attraction (see transcript discussion on regenerative capital flows, land-based projects, and Planetary Party governance).

### Recommended Sequencing

**Phase 0 (Now -- before Google Ads):**
- Audit and inventory existing content
- Identify and fill critical gaps (especially video and bioregion-specific packages)
- Build landing pages for top 3--4 bioregions
- Set up newsletter infrastructure and welcome sequence
- Launch Live with Syd (generates ongoing content)

**Phase 1 (Google Ads launch):**
- Start with newsletter-building campaigns using existing strong stories
- Schedule repurposed content across 4--6 weeks of newsletters so new subscribers have consistent engagement from day one

## 7. Community Building & Volunteer Attraction

### Digital Communities

- **WhatsApp community** -- High open rates, direct access. Many people in Syd's existing email lists don't open emails but check WhatsApp regularly. Build a Kinship Earth WhatsApp community and promote via Google Ads and newsletter.
- **Facebook page + Facebook community group** -- Separate from the page; a group creates peer-to-peer engagement. Drive some Google Ad traffic directly to the community.
- **Chatbot automation** -- When people engage on social media posts (comment a keyword), auto-send a DM with a link to subscribe, donate, or join the community. Thatcher noted these bots are very effective and currently popular.

### Volunteer Pipeline

- **Google Ads for volunteer recruitment** -- Run specific campaigns targeting people searching for volunteer opportunities in environmental/regenerative work.
- **Clear volunteer onboarding path** -- Use HubSpot to create a volunteer pipeline: interest form > welcome email > orientation info > matched to opportunity > active volunteer.
- **Public volunteer opportunity board** -- Instead of form submissions sitting in a queue (the current Permatours problem), create a public-facing board (Notion page or website section) where volunteer requests and opportunities are visible to the wider community.
- **Volunteer CRM tracking** -- HubSpot can manage volunteer relationships alongside donors, with different pipelines and properties.
