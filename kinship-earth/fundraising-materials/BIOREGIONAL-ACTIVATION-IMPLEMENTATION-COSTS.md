# Bioregional Activation Series -- Implementation Costs

**Kinship Earth | March 2026**

---

## Summary

This document outlines the estimated costs involved in implementing the Bioregional Activation Series across both the Northeast Turtle Island and Mexico activations. Costs are organized by category and phase. All figures are estimates and will vary by bioregion based on local context, scale, and partner contributions.

---

## 1. People & Facilitation

| Line Item | Per Activation | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Lead co-stewards (2) | $15,000--$25,000 | Stipends for primary organizers across all three phases |
| Facilitators & process designers | $10,000--$20,000 | Skilled facilitation for governance education, design labs, conflict transformation |
| Healing & conflict transformation practitioners | $5,000--$10,000 | On-call and responsive support across gatherings |
| Artists, musicians & culture bearers | $5,000--$15,000 | Commissions, honoraria, and material costs for creative and ceremonial contributions |
| Indigenous knowledge holders & elders | $3,000--$8,000 | Honoraria, travel, and hosting for place-based wisdom keepers |
| Mapping & research support | $3,000--$8,000 | Bioregional mapping, stakeholder research, relationship documentation |
| Administrative & coordination support | $5,000--$10,000 | Logistics, communications, scheduling, follow-up |

**Subtotal (per activation): $46,000--$96,000**

## Notes

- These estimates do not include Kinship Earth's core operational costs, which are funded separately.
- Many costs can be offset by in-kind contributions from partner organizations, anchor sites, and technology partners already in relationship.
- Flow Fund seed capital is the most variable line item and depends on fundraising capacity and funder alignment.
- Costs for the Mexico activation may differ due to local pricing, currency exchange, and the distinct logistical context.
- The capital strategy outlined in the deck ($2M by end of 2026) encompasses both the activation series and broader Kinship Earth operations including ongoing Flow Funder cohorts.
