## What Reduces Costs Over Time

- **Content library compounds** -- existing footage gets reused and remixed across campaigns
- **Local filmmakers build capacity** -- less onboarding, more autonomous capture
- **Templates and brand assets are reusable** -- one-time investment in Year 1
- **Flow Funder self-recording improves** -- coaching and equipment guidelines reduce need for professional shoots
- **Bioregional groups contribute content** -- as storytelling culture builds, community-sourced footage supplements professional production

## What Could Increase Costs

- **Full-length documentary production** -- a 45-90 min film across bioregions would add $50,000-$150,000+ depending on scope
- **Film festival submissions and distribution** -- $2,000-$10,000 for festival strategy, submission fees, and premiere events
- **International travel for film crews** -- Mexico and future international bioregions involve higher travel costs
- **Translation and multilingual versioning** -- essential for Mexico, Colombia, East Africa; adds $1,000-$5,000 per bioregion
- **Dedicated social media content creator** -- if volume of output requires a separate role, $20,000-$40,000/year

---

## Recommended Starting Point

**Mid-range scenario for Year 1: ~$145,000**

This gets professional-quality storytelling infrastructure across two bioregions with a part-time coordinator, fair compensation for local filmmakers, and a reusable content system. It produces enough content to meaningfully support fundraising for both Kinship Earth and the bioregional groups -- and sets up the flywheel for Year 2 expansion.

This investment should be viewed against its fundraising return: compelling bioregional storytelling content is the single most effective tool for donor cultivation, foundation engagement, and public visibility. If storytelling content contributes to even one additional $50,000-$100,000 gift per year, the program pays for itself.
