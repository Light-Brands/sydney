## Slide 2 — The Core Question

**Who is already doing this work, what do they need, and how do we build the foundations to resource a thriving bioregion together?**

- Regenerative practitioners, land stewards, organizers, technologists, funders, and culture bearers are arriving at the same conclusions independently
- Communities carry deep place-based knowledge but lack the coordination tools, governance literacy, and financial mechanisms to act at bioregional scale
- Overlapping infrastructure is being built without visibility into each other's efforts

---

## Slide 3 — What Is a Bioregional Activation?

A co-created gathering process -- not a conference or a program to be delivered.

- A living process of listening, mapping, and weaving
- Meets each bioregion where it is; builds from what already exists
- Rooted in bioregional organizing, regenerative finance, land-based community building, governance education, and culture-making
- The pillars remain consistent; the expression is always local

---

## Slide 4 — What Each Activation Produces

Tangible outcomes that emerge through relationship, not top-down design:

1. **Bioregional Flow Fund** -- trust-based, participatory funding mechanism where communities closest to the work decide where capital flows
2. **Bioregional Currency / Token** -- practical coordination and exchange tool with governance layers, reducing dependency on extractive financial systems
3. **Development capacity for land-based hubs** -- strengthening farms, community centers, bioregional finance facilities, and project sites already doing the work
4. **Commitment pooling** -- coordinating time, skills, relationships, land access, and funding across the bioregion
5. **Bioregional festival** -- a leave-a-positive-trace celebration co-created through the Planetary Party Protocol, leaving lasting regenerative infrastructure

---

## Slide 5 — The Six Pillars

Visual: Six interwoven pillars (web or hexagonal diagram)

1. **Relationship-Building & Mapping** -- Who is already stewarding land, capital, governance, culture, or exchange? Where are the natural synergies? Coherence over speed.
2. **Governance Education** -- Shared language around decision-making; exposure to participatory and commons-based models; co-creating across difference.
3. **Tools, Tech & Frameworks** -- Mapping tools, participatory governance frameworks, regenerative finance infrastructure, storytelling platforms; inviting builders into the room.
4. **Healing & Conflict Transformation** -- Available responsively, not centered; skilled facilitation for acknowledgment and integration without derailing the broader work.
5. **Honoring Place** -- Grounding each activation in Indigenous traditions and living ecologies of the specific bioregion.
6. **Culture, Arts & Creative Expression** -- Music, visual art, storytelling, and ceremony as cultural infrastructure -- supporting shared identity and imaginative vision.

---

## Slide 6 — How It Unfolds: Three Phases

Visual: Timeline or flow diagram showing the three phases

**Phase 1: Online Preparation Sessions**
- Introduce purpose and shared language
- Orient to bioregional context
- Advance mapping so a living picture of the field exists before gathering

**Phase 2: In-Person Gatherings at Anchor Sites**
- Embodied relationship-building and sensemaking
- Governance practice and storytelling
- Design labs translating mapped insights into next steps

**Phase 3: Distributed Hub Gatherings**
- Partner-hosted activations at farms, community centers, and land-based hubs
- Locally relevant expressions connected back to shared learning and coordination
