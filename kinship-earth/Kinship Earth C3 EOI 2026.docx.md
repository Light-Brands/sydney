## **Focus: Barriers to Catalytic Capital Deployment**

Catalytic philanthropic capital \- unrestricted grants, DAF distributions, foundation payouts, and family office giving \- remains one of the most underleveraged tools in the impact finance ecosystem. Despite growing interest in trust-based, participatory approaches, 3 persistent barriers prevent this capital from reaching grassroots communities and bioregional groups at scale:

1. **No replicable deployment infrastructure.** Philanthropists and foundations interested in trust-based, community-directed grantmaking lack a practical, modular framework to launch and operate such vehicles. Each effort starts from scratch, duplicating legal, operational, and relational design work.  
2. **Untapped capital providers remain on the sidelines.** DAFs hold over $230 billion in assets with no payout requirement. Family offices and high-net-worth individuals increasingly want to fund grassroots regeneration but lack on-ramps. These providers need actionable models \- not theory \- to move capital.  
3. **Grassroots communities are structurally excluded.** Traditional grantmaking’s application-driven, compliance-heavy process locks out the most effective actors. Indigenous peoples protect 80% of the world’s biodiversity yet receive less than 1% of climate finance. Community organizations operating on under $50K/year consistently deliver outsized impact but cannot access institutional philanthropy.

## **Proposed Solution: Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook & Deployment Platform**

Kinship Earth proposes to develop, test, and publish the Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook \- a free, open-access, modular toolkit that enables communities, foundations, and grantors to launch bioregional Flow Funds. The Playbook creates a replicable deployment mechanism for catalytic philanthropic capital that expands the pool of providers \- giving DAFs, family offices, and foundations a field-tested pathway to deploy capital through trust-based, community-directed grantmaking.

**Why this is needed and how it goes beyond what currently exists**: Trust-based philanthropy is growing as a concept (e.g., the Trust-Based Philanthropy Project), but the field lacks a practical, deployable vehicle design \- especially one organized around bioregions rather than political boundaries or issue silos. Kinship Earth’s Flow Funding model has been field-tested across 12+ bioregions globally with $370,000+ deployed to 23 Flow Funders since 2024, demonstrating that the model works. What is missing is the infrastructure layer to make it replicable: modular legal templates, governance frameworks, lightweight accountability systems, and donor onboarding tools \- all packaged in a single open-access resource that others can adapt and deploy.

**The Playbook directly addresses C3’s intersection of supply and deployment**: it is both a deployment on-ramp (enabling faster, lower-cost fund launches) and a supply-side tool (converting interested-but-inactive capital holders into active catalytic capital providers).

# **Proposed Activities & Anticipated Results**

* **Codify the Flow Funding model into modular Playbook components**: legal/compliance templates, governance frameworks, Flow Funder selection processes, lightweight reporting structures, and Community of Practice facilitation guides \- all designed for adaptation across geographies, cultures, and capital types.  
* Field-test with 3 \- 5 bioregional communities designing their own Flow Funds (drawing from Kinship Earth’s collaborations in Cascadia, Northeast Turtle Island, Jamaica, Colombia, & East Africa), incorporating their feedback for cross-cultural applicability.  
* Develop DAF & family office onboarding tools that translate the Flow Funding model into actionable steps for new capital providers \- including case studies, impact narratives, and a decision framework for donors evaluating trust-based giving vehicles.  
* Publish under Creative Commons license and disseminate through virtual workshops (3 \- 4 inclusive convenings), and partner networks to maximize adoption.

**Anticipated Results & KPIs:**

* **KPI 1**: 3 \- 5 bioregional communities using the Playbook to design or launch their own Flow Funds within 12 months.  
* **KPI 2:** $500K \- $1M in new catalytic capital committed or deployed through Playbook \- enabled Flow Funds by project end.  
* **KPI 3:** 10+ new DAFs, family offices, or foundations engaged through onboarding and workshops.  
* **Systemic impact:** An open-source, field-tested infrastructure layer that reduces the time-to-launch for trust-based philanthropic vehicles from months to weeks, enabling replication by any aligned community or capital provider globally.
