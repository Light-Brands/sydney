## About Kinship Earth

Kinship Earth is a private foundation practicing Flow Funding — a trust-based, participatory grantmaking model that moves unrestricted capital directly to grassroots leaders and bioregional organizers. Since mid-2024, we have deployed $370K+ to 23 Flow Funders across 12+ bioregions through 3 cohorts — each organized around interconnected themes including bioregional organizing, food sovereignty, Indigenous rights, ecosystem restoration, and community resilience. We have developed a successful, field-tested model and now seek to refine it, codify it, and support its broader adoption and replicability.

## Why Supply & Facilitating Deployment

This proposal operates at the intersection of catalytic capital supply and deployment. On the deployment side, our Playbooks provide replicable, modular infrastructure — legal templates, governance frameworks, and operational guides — that reduce time-to-launch for trust-based vehicles from months to weeks. On the supply side, our funder onboarding tools and decision frameworks convert inactive capital holders (DAFs, family offices, foundations) into active catalytic capital providers. The result is a single open-access resource that both channels capital efficiently into deployable community-level deals and expands the pool of providers doing so.

## Barriers the Project Seeks to Overcome

Catalytic philanthropic capital — unrestricted grants, DAF distributions, foundation payouts, and family office giving — is critically underleveraged. Three field-wide barriers prevent it from reaching grassroots communities at scale:

1. **No replicable deployment infrastructure.** Each trust-based grantmaking vehicle is built from scratch — duplicating legal structuring, governance, and operations. This inflates costs, extends timelines, and limits the number of vehicles that reach deployment.
2. **Untapped capital providers remain on the sidelines.** DAFs hold $230B+ with no payout requirement. Family offices and local investors want to fund grassroots regeneration but lack actionable on-ramps. Without field-tested models, willing capital sits idle.
3. **Grassroots communities are structurally excluded.** Compliance-heavy grantmaking locks out the most effective actors. Indigenous peoples protect 80% of biodiversity yet receive <1% of climate finance. The current system channels capital toward grant-writing capacity, not proximity to impact.

## Proposed Solution

Kinship Earth will finalize, field-test, and publish two complementary open-access resources: (1) a **Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook** for bioregional communities — currently in draft — providing modular tools to launch and govern their own Flow Funds; and (2) a **Bioregional Flow Fueling Playbook** for funders — currently in development — offering onboarding pathways, decision frameworks, and deployment guides for DAFs, family offices, and foundations seeking to provide catalytic capital.

**Why it's needed:** Trust-based philanthropy is gaining traction conceptually, but the field lacks practical, deployable vehicle designs — especially ones organized around bioregions, defined by watersheds, ecosystems, and cultures rather than political boundaries. The bioregional organizers in our existing cohorts have told us directly that they wish they had this kind of structured guidance when they started. Our legal agreements are internationally recognized and can be used or adapted across jurisdictions, including all countries on OFAC's sanctioned list (we only fund in countries where we are legally permitted to operate).

## Activities & Anticipated Results

**Activities:** Finalize both Playbooks into modular components (legal templates, governance frameworks, selection processes, reporting structures, funder onboarding tools); field-test with one bioregion from each of our three existing cohorts — communities we have already resourced and who are embedded in our network; incorporate partner and community feedback; publish under Creative Commons; disseminate through 3–4 virtual convenings and partner networks.

**KPIs:** 3–5 communities using the Playbook to design/launch Flow Funds within 12 months · $500K–$1M in new catalytic capital committed or deployed through Playbook-enabled funds · 10+ new capital providers engaged, with at least 3 making initial commitments
