## **Focus: Barriers the Project Seeks to Overcome**

Catalytic philanthropic capital — unrestricted grants, DAF distributions, foundation payouts, and family office giving — is one of the most underleveraged tools in the impact finance ecosystem. Three persistent, field-wide barriers prevent this capital from reaching grassroots communities and bioregional organizations at scale:

1. **No replicable deployment infrastructure.** Philanthropists and foundations interested in trust-based, community-directed grantmaking lack a practical, modular framework to design and launch such vehicles. Each effort starts from scratch — duplicating legal structuring, governance design, accountability frameworks, and operational setup. This duplication inflates transaction costs, extends time-to-launch from months to years, and limits the number of vehicles that reach deployment.
2. **Untapped capital providers remain on the sidelines.** DAFs hold over $230 billion in assets with no payout requirement. Family offices, high-net-worth individuals, and local investors increasingly want to fund grassroots regeneration but lack clear on-ramps. These providers need field-tested, actionable models — not theory — to move capital. Without structured pathways and decision tools, willing capital sits idle.
3. **Grassroots communities are structurally excluded from capital flows.** Traditional grantmaking's application-driven, compliance-heavy processes lock out the most effective actors. Indigenous peoples protect 80% of the world's biodiversity yet receive less than 1% of climate finance. Community organizations operating on under $50K/year consistently deliver outsized impact but cannot access institutional philanthropy. The current infrastructure channels catalytic capital toward organizations with grant-writing capacity, not those with the greatest proximity to impact.

## **Proposed Solution: The Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook**

Kinship Earth proposes to finalize, field-test, and publish the **Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook** — a free, open-access, modular toolkit that enables communities, foundations, and capital providers to launch bioregional Flow Funds. The Playbook creates replicable deployment infrastructure for catalytic philanthropic capital while simultaneously expanding the pool of providers — giving DAFs, family offices, foundations, and local investors a field-tested pathway to deploy capital through trust-based, community-directed grantmaking.

**Why this is needed and how it goes beyond what currently exists:** Trust-based philanthropy is gaining traction as a concept (e.g., the Trust-Based Philanthropy Project), but the field lacks a practical, deployable vehicle design — especially one organized around bioregions rather than political boundaries or issue silos. Kinship Earth's Flow Funding model has been field-tested across 12+ bioregions globally, with $800,000+ raised and $370,000+ deployed to 23 Flow Funders across 3 cohorts since mid-2024. Deployment has spanned Cascadia, the Greater Tkaronto Bioregion, the Northeast of Turtle Island, Colombia, Jamaica, Mexico, and East Africa — demonstrating cross-geographic and cross-cultural applicability. What is missing is the infrastructure layer to make this replicable at field level: modular legal templates, governance frameworks, Flow Funder selection processes, lightweight accountability systems, and capital provider onboarding tools — all packaged in a single open-access resource that any aligned community or investor can adapt and deploy.

**The Playbook operates at the intersection of supply and deployment.** It is both a **deployment mechanism** — reducing time-to-launch for trust-based philanthropic vehicles from months to weeks, lowering transaction costs, and simplifying structuring through modular templates — and a **supply-side tool** — converting interested-but-inactive capital holders (DAFs, family offices, foundations, local investors) into active catalytic capital providers through onboarding pathways, decision frameworks, and impact case studies. The vehicle design aggregates catalytic capital from new sources and channels it directly into community-directed deployment.

## **Proposed Activities & Anticipated Results**

**Activities:**

* **Finalize the Playbook into field-ready, modular components** — legal/compliance templates (covering fiscal sponsorship, donor-advised fund structuring, and independent foundation pathways), governance frameworks, Flow Funder selection and vetting processes, lightweight story-based reporting structures, and Community of Practice facilitation guides — all designed for adaptation across geographies, cultures, and capital types.
* **Field-test with 3–5 bioregional communities** designing their own Flow Funds (drawing from active collaborations in Cascadia, Northeast Turtle Island, Jamaica, Colombia, and Mexico), incorporating feedback to ensure cross-cultural and cross-jurisdictional applicability.
* **Develop capital provider onboarding tools** that translate the Flow Funding model into actionable steps for DAFs, family offices, foundations, and local investors — including impact case studies, a donor decision framework, and structured partnership templates for integrating catalytic capital into existing giving vehicles.
* **Publish under Creative Commons license** and disseminate through 3–4 inclusive virtual convenings, partner networks (including Regenerate Cascadia, Regenerosity, bioregional organizing groups in Latin America and the Caribbean), and direct engagement with donor advisors and intermediary platforms to maximize adoption and replication.

**Anticipated Results & KPIs:**

* **KPI 1:** 3–5 bioregional communities actively using the Playbook to design or launch their own Flow Funds within 12 months.
* **KPI 2:** $500K–$1M in new catalytic capital committed or deployed through Playbook-enabled Flow Funds by project end.
* **KPI 3:** 10+ new capital providers (DAFs, family offices, foundations, or local investors) engaged through onboarding tools and convenings, with at least 3 making initial commitments.
* **Systemic impact:** A free, open-source, field-tested infrastructure layer that materially reduces the cost, complexity, and time required to launch trust-based philanthropic vehicles — enabling replication by any aligned community or capital provider across geographies, sectors, and investor types. The Playbook is designed as a scalable market-level solution: each new Flow Fund launched becomes both a deployment channel and a supply-side node that attracts and aggregates additional catalytic capital into the ecosystem.

## **Relevant Experience & Key Partnerships**

Kinship Earth is a 501(c)(3) private foundation that has deployed $370,000+ in unrestricted flow funding grants to 23 Flow Funders across 12+ bioregions since launching the model in mid-2024. The organization has designed, deployed, and iterated on trust-based grantmaking infrastructure across diverse geographies — spanning North America, Latin America, the Caribbean, and East Africa — and has built a Community of Practice (launched October 2024) connecting Flow Funders, donors, and bioregional organizers. Kinship Earth carries the direct lineage of Marion Rockefeller Weber, the pioneer of the flow funding concept (early 1990s), who serves as advisor. Co-founder Susan Davis Moora was one of Weber's original flow funding recipients.

[Sydney Griffith](https://www.kinshipearth.org), Executive Director — vision, strategy, fundraising, and systems design; supported 40+ land-based projects.
