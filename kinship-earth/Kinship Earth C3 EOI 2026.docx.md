## Barriers the Project Seeks to Overcome

Catalytic philanthropic capital — unrestricted grants, DAF distributions, foundation payouts, and family office giving — is critically underleveraged. Three field-wide barriers prevent it from reaching grassroots communities at scale:

1. **No replicable deployment infrastructure.** Each trust-based grantmaking vehicle is built from scratch — duplicating legal structuring, governance, and operations. This inflates costs, extends timelines from months to years, and limits the number of vehicles that reach deployment.
2. **Untapped capital providers remain on the sidelines.** DAFs hold $230B+ with no payout requirement. Family offices and local investors want to fund grassroots regeneration but lack clear, actionable on-ramps. Without field-tested models, willing capital sits idle.
3. **Grassroots communities are structurally excluded.** Compliance-heavy grantmaking locks out the most effective actors. Indigenous peoples protect 80% of biodiversity yet receive <1% of climate finance. The current system channels capital toward grant-writing capacity, not proximity to impact.

## Proposed Solution

Kinship Earth will finalize, field-test, and publish the **Bioregional Flow Funding Playbook** — a free, open-access, modular toolkit enabling communities, foundations, and capital providers to launch bioregional Flow Funds.

**Why it's needed and how it goes beyond what exists:** Trust-based philanthropy is gaining traction conceptually (e.g., Trust-Based Philanthropy Project), but the field lacks a practical, deployable vehicle design — especially one organized around bioregions. Kinship Earth's model has been field-tested across 12+ bioregions, with $800K+ raised and $370K+ deployed to 23 Flow Funders across 3 cohorts since mid-2024. What's missing is the infrastructure layer to make this replicable: modular legal templates, governance frameworks, accountability systems, and capital provider onboarding tools — all in a single open-access resource.

The Playbook operates at the intersection of supply and deployment — it reduces time-to-launch for trust-based vehicles from months to weeks (deployment), while converting inactive capital holders into active providers through onboarding pathways and decision frameworks (supply).

## Activities & Anticipated Results

**Activities:** Finalize Playbook into modular components (legal templates, governance frameworks, selection processes, reporting structures); field-test with 3–5 bioregional communities; develop capital provider onboarding tools for DAFs, family offices, and foundations; publish under Creative Commons and disseminate through 3–4 virtual convenings and partner networks.

**KPIs:**
- 3–5 communities using the Playbook to design/launch Flow Funds within 12 months
- $500K–$1M in new catalytic capital committed or deployed through Playbook-enabled funds
- 10+ new capital providers engaged, with at least 3 making initial commitments

## Relevant Experience & Key Partnerships

Kinship Earth has deployed $370K+ in unrestricted flow funding to 23 Flow Funders across 12+ bioregions since mid-2024, spanning North America, Latin America, the Caribbean, and East Africa. The organization carries the direct lineage of Marion Rockefeller Weber, pioneer of the flow funding concept (early 1990s), who serves as advisor. Co-founder Susan Davis Moora was one of Weber's original flow funding recipients.
