## Detailed Research

---

### 1. Vanguard Foundation (San Francisco)

**Status: CLOSED — No longer operating**

The Vanguard Public Foundation was a pioneering San Francisco-based social justice philanthropy established in 1972 by a group of young inheritors. It funded grassroots organizations across progressive causes for nearly four decades before closing in 2011 following a major financial fraud scandal. It was part of the Funding Exchange network and inspired similar foundations like Haymarket People's Fund and Liberty Hill Foundation.

- **Verdict:** Not a viable funding source — organization no longer exists.

---

### 2. Tides Foundation

- **Website:** <https://www.tides.org>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** No

Tides Foundation is not a traditional grantmaker. It holds over 400 donor-advised funds and provides fiscal sponsorship to 80+ organizations. Grants are made based on partner recommendations only.

**How to potentially access funding:**
- Partner with a Tides entity through their "Partner with Us" form at tides.org
- Watch for occasional RFPs through their newsletter
- Get recommended by an existing Tides partner

**Relevant pooled funds:** Women's Environmental Leadership Fund, Frontline Justice Fund

**KE alignment:** Moderate — their fiscal sponsorship and pooled fund model could be relevant, but direct grants are unlikely without an existing relationship.

---

### 3. MacArthur Foundation

- **Website:** <https://www.macfound.org/info-grantseekers/>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** Technically yes, but very rarely funds them

The MacArthur Foundation generally does not fund unsolicited proposals, though it does review them. Very few are selected. Their Climate Solutions program is not currently accepting unsolicited proposals. Their Big Bets programs are winding down and will formally end in 2026.

**Notable developments:**
- In February 2025, MacArthur announced it is increasing its annual payout rate to at least 6% for the next two years in response to federal funding cuts under the Trump administration.
- The **100&Change** competition — a single $100 million grant — is open to any proposed solution to any critical problem. Worth monitoring for future rounds.

**KE alignment:** Moderate — climate focus is relevant but the scale and approach may not match KE's grassroots model. The foundation is in a transition period as Big Bets wind down. New grantmaking priorities may emerge.

### 5. Threshold Foundation

- **Website:** <https://www.thresholdfoundation.org>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** Not openly, but outreach is encouraged

Threshold Foundation is a membership-based organization established in 1981. Members gather monthly to administer grantmaking through Funding Circles focused on specific causes.

**Current Funding Circles:**
- **Land and Climate** — strong alignment with KE
- **Black Justice Flow Fund** — strong alignment with KE
- Middle East Peace
- Fair Elections
- Immigration Justice
- High Impact Documentary Films
- Psychedelic Research, Education and Policy

**Grant size:** $10,000–$200,000 per grant; over $2 million distributed annually

**Approach:**
- All grantmaking is conducted through a racial equity and climate change lens
- Described as a "responsive and collaborative funder that works to help its grantees thrive"
- Proactive in giving — organizations need to get on their radar

**KE alignment: HIGH** — The Land and Climate program, Black Justice Flow Fund, racial equity lens, and grassroots focus are very well-aligned with Kinship Earth's mission. The membership-based trust model mirrors KE's own approach.

**Recommended action:** Email to introduce KE and its Land & Climate + flow funding work.

### 6. Haymarket People's Fund

- **Website:** <https://www.haymarket.org/grants-1>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** Yes

**Grant types:**
- **Sustaining Grants** — up to $20,000 per organization; cycle typically opens in fall
- **Urgent Response Grants** — rolling basis while funding is available

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Must be active in the 6 New England states (CT, ME, MA, RI, NH, VT)
- Budget must be under $400,000
- Priority given to people of color-led organizations incorporating anti-racism values

**How to apply:**
- New applicants: Contact Jaime at jaime@haymarket.org or 617-522-7676 ext. 115
- Schedule a phone conversation first to assess alignment
- Grant information webinar available on their site

**KE alignment:** Moderate — strong alignment on values (grassroots, community-led, racial justice), but geographic limitation to New England only is the main constraint. If KE has projects or flow fund recipients in the U.S. Northeast, this could be a fit.

### 7. Council of Foundations

- **Website:** <https://www.cof.org>
- **Status:** Not a grantmaker

The Council on Foundations is a membership and resource organization for foundations — it helps foundations operate effectively but does not itself give grants or accept applications. It is essentially a trade association for the philanthropic sector.

**Potential use:** Networking resource to connect with aligned foundations, or a directory to find community foundations in relevant regions.

- **Verdict:** Not a funding source for Kinship Earth.

### 8. Rockefeller Brothers Fund (RBF)

- **Website:** <https://www.rbf.org/grants/for-grant-seekers>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** Yes, via online portal — but less than 1% of unsolicited applications result in funding

RBF staff are highly engaged in their fields and actively identify organizations. They accept Letters of Inquiry through their online portal.

**Relevant program — Sustainable Development:**
- Addresses climate change and biodiversity loss
- Supports development that is "ecologically based, economically sound, socially just, culturally appropriate, and consistent with intergenerational equity"
- U.S. focus: building green economy at federal, state, and local levels
- International focus includes Central America (soil resilience, water security)

**How to apply:**
- Review program guidelines and recent grants at rbf.org
- Submit through online portal (no mail, email, or fax accepted)
- Decision typically within 3 months

**KE alignment:** Moderate–High — sustainable development language overlaps with KE's mission. RBF's focus tends toward policy and institutional-level change rather than grassroots flow funding, but an LOI is still worth submitting.

### 9. Rockefeller Family Fund (RFF)

- **Contact:** info@rffund.org
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** No

RFF does not accept unsolicited proposals or letters of inquiry. They identify new grantees from within their networks and solicit proposals only after concluding an organization has a reasonable chance of funding.

**Program areas:** Climate, Democracy, and Economic Justice for Women

**How to get on their radar:**
- Send a brief email description of your organization to info@rffund.org
- They are "always open to learning about creative and effective work" aligned with their goals

**KE alignment:** Moderate — Climate focus is relevant, but their action-oriented policy advocacy focus may not perfectly match KE's community-based flow funding model.

### 10. Black Farmer Fund

- **Website:** <https://blackfarmerfund.org>
- **Accepts applications:** Periodically

Black Farmer Fund was formed out of conversations among Black farmers in the Northeast who were seeking capital that does not replicate discriminatory and predatory lending practices. It provides low-interest community notes and gifts to businesses strengthening the Black food system.

**Who they support:** Farmers, herbalists, restaurant owners, caterers, food distributors, and other food-related entrepreneurs across the Northeast.

**Current programs:** Fund 2.0 and Rapid Response Fund

**How to stay informed:** Sign up for their newsletter at blackfarmerfund.org

**KE alignment: HIGH** — Very strong alignment with KE's values around trust-based, community-led, BIPOC-centered food system work. KE's flow funders and bioregional partners in the Northeast could potentially connect with or be supported by this fund.

### 11. Potlikker Capital

*Note: Listed as "Potlucker Capital" — likely refers to Potlikker Capital.*

- **Website:** <https://potlikkercapital.com>
- **Accepts applications:** Yes — for farmer borrowers

Potlikker Capital is a California-based 501(c)(3) charitable loan fund founded in 2020, integrating technical, social, and blended financial capital to "reparatively" invest in regenerative BIPOC farmers in community clusters across the nation.

**What they offer:**
- Non-extractive investments
- Zero and low-interest loans
- Grants and recoverable grants
- Integrated capital model (loans + grants + strategic support)
- Governed by farm community members

**Investment products:** "Jubilee Notes" for non-accredited investors, "Justice Notes" and "Philanthropic Notes" for accredited investors

**KE alignment: HIGH** — Their integrated capital, reparative justice model is very aligned with KE's flow funding philosophy. Could serve as both a resource for KE's farmer partners and a model/ally for KE's approach to regenerative finance.

### 12. DIRT Capital Partners

- **Website:** <https://www.dirtpartners.com>
- **Accepts applications:** Yes — for farmer partners

DIRT Capital Partners invests in farmland in partnership with experienced regenerative farmers throughout the U.S., prioritizing impact over financial return. Founded in 2013 with approximately $40M in assets under management.

**What they offer:**
- Flexible, customized lease-to-own arrangements
- Conservation easement partnerships
- Collaboration with nonprofits and foundations for affordable land access
- Focus on wealth-building for farmers, especially BIPOC farmers

**Impact highlights:**
- 74% of projects deploy cover cropping
- $8.1M invested in projects with BIPOC farmer ownership
- $15.7M invested in projects with farmers under 40
- Active in Northeast and Mid-Atlantic, expanding nationwide

**KE alignment: HIGH** — Very strong alignment for KE's land-based regenerative projects, especially in the Northeast. Could be a partner for farmland access for KE's flow fund recipients.

### 13. Builders Vision (Lukas Walton)

- **Website:** <https://www.buildersvision.com>
- **Accepts unsolicited proposals:** No — invitation and relationship-based only

Builders Vision is the investment and philanthropy platform of Lukas Walton (grandson of Walmart founder Sam Walton), managing over $15 billion across multiple asset classes. Based in Chicago with approximately 100 staff.

**Scale of grantmaking:**
- $168M granted in 2022; $199M in 2023
- $180M+ to food and agriculture grantees since founding
- 90% of $1B endowment in mission-related investments
- Nearly twice the size of Walton Family Foundation's environmental program

**Focus areas:** Food & Agriculture, Climate & Energy, Oceans, Community

**Regenerative agriculture focus:** No-till, crop rotation, cover cropping, livestock integration, agroforestry, plant-based alternatives, transformative regenerative systems

**How to access (difficult):**
- Not easily accessible to new grantees and smaller organizations
- Need a connection to a staff member
- Networking on social media may help
- Organizations need work that can scale regionally and provide data on effectiveness

**Key connection:** Builders Vision was an early investor in Mad Capital's Perennial Fund II — indicating they are already investing in the regenerative agriculture finance ecosystem that includes several of KE's potential partners.

**KE alignment: VERY HIGH** on mission, but accessibility is the challenge. KE's connections to Mad Capital, DIRT Capital, or Potlikker Capital could create a pathway.

---

### 14. Mad Capital

- **Website:** <https://www.madagriculture.org>
- **Accepts applications:** Yes — for farmer borrowers

Mad Capital provides tailor-fit private credit to mid-sized regenerative organic farmers in the U.S., bridging the transition financing gap.

**Perennial Fund II (closed September 2025):**
- Closed at $78.4M (initially targeting $25M)
- 7x larger than the $10M Fund I
- Total AUM now $100M
- Backed by Builders Vision, Rockefeller Foundation, Schmidt Family Foundation
- 111 investors

**What they offer:**
- Custom-fit loans based on each farm's growth plans
- Financing for farmers at all stages of regenerative transition
- Covers soil health investments, market development, enterprise diversification
- Working with 117,297 acres across 16 states

**Looking ahead:** Fund III in conceptualization — targeting $500M+ evergreen fund

**KE alignment: HIGH** — Could be a financing partner for farmers in KE's bioregional network who need transition capital. Also a potential ally and model for KE's approach to regenerative finance.
