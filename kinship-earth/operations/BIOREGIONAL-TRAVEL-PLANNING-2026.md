## 1. Bogota, Colombia

Colombia is an **active PPP bioregion since Q1 2024** with forest/watershed restoration and regenerative governance across 5 regions. Bioregional storytelling here captures Flow Funder impact, indigenous governance, and ecological restoration work.

### Weather

Bogota sits at 2,640m elevation with two dry and two wet seasons:

| Season | Months | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Dry ("Verano") 1** | Mid-Dec -- late Feb | Best weather, clear skies, 40-50mm rain/month |
| **Wet ("Invierno") 1** | Late Mar -- early Jun | 100-110mm rain, 55-65% daily rain probability |
| **Dry ("Veranillo") 2** | Jul -- Aug | Second-best weather window |
| **Wet ("Invierno") 2** | Late Sep -- early Dec | Heaviest rains |

Temps range 8-18C (46-64F) year-round. Pack layers + light waterproof. High UV at altitude -- sunscreen essential.

### PPP-Aligned Events

| Event | Dates | PPP Alignment |
|---|---|---|
| **Estereo Picnic** | Mar 20-22 | Celebration as catalyst; sustainability-committed festival with art installations, eco-stages. Aligns with CELEBRATE phase. |
| **Ibero-American Theater Festival (FITB)** | Mar 20 -- Apr 5 | Culture, arts & creative expression; international performances transform streets into stages. Storytelling infrastructure. |
| **FILBo (International Book Fair)** | Apr 21 -- May 4 | Narrative infrastructure; 630K+ attendees, cross-cultural literary dialogue. |
| **EcoFestivales / Festivales al Parque** | Various (summer) | Direct sustainability alignment -- Bogota's city-backed program integrating environmental awareness into massive cultural events. Aligns with REGENERATE phase. |
| **Rock al Parque** | TBD (typically Jul) | Free 300K-person festival at Simon Bolivar Park. Community gathering + positive-trace potential. |
| **Colombian Crafts Fair** | Aug 5-15 | Ancestral techniques (Wayuu weaving, Raquira ceramics). Honoring place + indigenous knowledge. |
| **Bogota Film Festival** | Sep 20-26 | Documentary/film screening opportunity for bioregional storytelling content. |

**Best PPP storytelling window: March 20 -- April 5.** The overlap of Estereo Picnic (sustainability-focused music festival) and the Theater Festival creates a concentrated cultural moment for filming, convening, and bioregional storytelling capture. This also coincides with Semana Santa (Easter week Apr 13-20), which adds domestic cultural richness.

### Flight Costs (from US)

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cheapest months** | March, April, September |
| **Lowest fares found** | $68-104 RT from Florida cities (Orlando, Fort Lauderdale, Miami) |
| **Budget airlines** | Spirit, LATAM, Avianca |
| **Avoid** | December (peak pricing, 4%+ premium) |
| **Booking tip** | 4+ weeks advance; Sunday bookings save 6-13% |

**March is the sweet spot** -- budget-friendly fares align perfectly with the Estereo Picnic / FITB storytelling window.

### Recommended Trip

**March 18-28, 2026** (10 days)
- Arrive ahead of Estereo Picnic (Mar 20-22) for setup and pre-event community convening
- Capture film at the festival and FITB opening (CELEBRATE phase)
- Spend remaining days visiting Flow Funder sites and bioregional restoration projects across the 5 active regions (SENSE/SEE phases)
- Estimated RT flight: $100-250 depending on departure city

## 2. Montego Bay, Jamaica

Jamaica is an **active PPP bioregion since Aug 2025** focused on post-hurricane recovery and Indigenous-led stewardship with Rastafari Indigenous Village. Storytelling here captures resilience, cultural heritage, land stewardship, and community-led recovery.

### Weather

| Season | Months | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Dry (Peak)** | Mid-Dec -- mid-Apr | Best weather, 82-84F, lowest rainfall |
| **Shoulder 1** | Mid-Apr -- Jun | Still sunny, better rates |
| **Wet/Hurricane** | Jul -- Oct | Hottest (86F Jul), afternoon rains, hurricane risk |
| **Shoulder 2** | Nov -- mid-Dec | Rain easing, uncrowded, great value |

Montego Bay north coast: ~85F average, 60 inches annual rainfall. Less rain than Negril.

### PPP-Aligned Events

| Event | Dates | PPP Alignment |
|---|---|---|
| **Accompong Maroon Festival** | Jan 6 | Indigenous sovereignty and governance -- commemorates 1739 Maroon peace treaty. Deeply aligned with SENSE and Honoring Place. |
| **Bob Marley Birthday Celebration** | ~Feb 6 (Kingston/Nine Mile) | Cultural heritage, music as movement infrastructure. CELEBRATE phase. |
| **Mobay Creative Arts Festival** | Mar 29 | Arts, culture, creative expression at Montego Bay Cultural Centre. Storytelling guild alignment. |
| **Reggae Sumfest** | Jul 13-19 | Caribbean's largest music festival, held in MoBay. Organizers actively pursuing sustainability (waste reduction, energy efficiency, community engagement). CELEBRATE + REGENERATE phases. |
| **MoBay Jerk Festival** | TBD (annual) | Food sovereignty, local cuisine, cultural identity. Community gathering. |
| **Dream Weekend** | Jul 30+ | Large-scale cultural gathering, arts and music. |
| **Jamaica Pro-Am Golf Tournament** | Nov 18-22 | Potential donor cultivation event -- high-net-worth attendees in MoBay. |

**Best PPP storytelling windows:**

1. **Late November -- early December** -- Shoulder season with great weather, low crowds, budget-friendly. Ideal for quieter, relationship-focused bioregional storytelling: visiting Rastafari Indigenous Village, documenting post-hurricane recovery, Flow Funder site visits. The Nov 18-22 golf tournament also brings potential aligned donors to MoBay.

2. **Mid-July** -- Reggae Sumfest (Jul 13-19) is the most PPP-aligned major event with active sustainability commitments. However: hurricane season, hotter, and flights are cheapest but weather is riskiest.

3. **Late January -- early February** -- Pair the Accompong Maroon Festival (Jan 6) with Bob Marley celebrations (~Feb 6) for a trip centered on Indigenous governance and cultural heritage. Excellent weather but peak pricing.

### Flight Costs (from US)

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cheapest months** | May, September, October |
| **Lowest fares found** | $206-224 RT from Fort Lauderdale/Atlanta |
| **Budget airlines** | Spirit, Frontier, Southwest, JetBlue |
| **Peak pricing** | Jan-Mar ($700+/night hotels possible) |
| **Booking tip** | 2-4 months advance; fly midweek (Mon/Tue/Wed); depart Thursday saves 16% |

**November is the value play** -- shoulder-season pricing ($250-350 RT) with excellent weather and the quieter atmosphere ideal for deep bioregional storytelling.

### Recommended Trips

**Option A: November 16-26, 2026** (10 days) -- *Best value + storytelling depth*
- Shoulder season weather/pricing sweet spot
- Visit Rastafari Indigenous Village for Indigenous-led stewardship documentation
- Post-hurricane recovery storytelling in communities
- Flow Funder site visits and relationship-building (SENSE/SEE)
- Overlap with Pro-Am tournament for donor cultivation opportunity

**Option B: January 4-12, 2026** (8 days) -- *Cultural heritage focus*
- Accompong Maroon Festival (Jan 6) -- Indigenous governance documentation
- Excellent dry-season weather
- Higher costs but unmatched cultural content for the Honoring Place pillar

## 3. Costa Rica

Costa Rica is listed as a **future activation bioregion** and is home to some of the world's most PPP-aligned sustainability events. It is a natural fit for bioregional storytelling given its global leadership in conservation, renewable energy, and regenerative tourism.

### Weather

| Season | Months | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| **Dry (Verano)** | Mid-Dec -- Apr | Best weather, 75-85F, minimal rain |
| **Green/Rainy 1** | May -- Jun | Afternoon showers, mornings sunny, lush landscapes |
| **Mini Dry (Veranillo)** | Jul | Brief sunshine pocket |
| **Green/Rainy 2** | Aug -- Nov | Heaviest rain Sep-Oct (12in/month) |

Regional note: Caribbean coast is **drier** in Sep-Oct (opposite of Pacific side). Multiple microclimates mean conditions vary dramatically by zone.

2026 note: El Nino may develop later in 2026, potentially bringing drier-than-normal conditions.

### PPP-Aligned Events

| Event | Dates | PPP Alignment |
|---|---|---|
| **Dreamsea Sonica** | Jan 23-24, Tamarindo | Boutique eco-conscious music festival in the jungle. Sustainability-committed (composting, reusables, nature respect). CELEBRATE phase. |
| **Envision Festival** | Feb 23 -- Mar 2, Uvita | **The most PPP-aligned event across all three destinations.** Music, art, wellness, and environmental action. 2026 edition caps at 5,500 attendees with expanded sustainability: compostable serveware, waterless composting toilets, reforestation programs, partnership with Somos El Cambio Foundation. Workshops on permaculture, regenerative living, consciousness exploration. Directly mirrors SENSE/CELEBRATE/REGENERATE phases. |

**Best PPP storytelling window: February 23 -- March 2 (Envision Festival)**

Envision is essentially a Planetary Party in practice -- it blends music, regenerative education, ecological action, and community. The 2026 edition's "Back to our Roots" theme and reduced capacity create an intimate setting ideal for:
- Filming permaculture and regenerative living workshops (aligned with Permatours' educational mission)
- Documenting sustainability infrastructure as a model for PPP positive-trace events
- Connecting with aligned practitioners, filmmakers, and funders in the regenerative space
- Capturing reforestation and community partnership stories (REGENERATE phase)

### Flight Costs (from US)

| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cheapest months** | May, August, September, October |
| **Average RT fare** | ~$239 (2026 forecast) |
| **Lowest fares found** | $81-100 RT on Spirit, Copa, Volaris Costa Rica |
| **January/February** | Sub-$200 deals possible on select routes |
| **Booking tip** | 2-6 months advance; fly Tue/Wed/Thu; book on Sundays |

**February is accessible** -- while technically dry/peak season, Costa Rica fares remain reasonable ($200-300 RT) compared to Caribbean destinations, and the Envision alignment makes it the clear choice.

### Recommended Trip

**February 20 -- March 4, 2026** (12 days)
- Arrive a few days early to explore Uvita bioregion and connect with local filmmakers
- Envision Festival (Feb 23 -- Mar 2) -- full immersion in PPP-aligned programming
- Document sustainability infrastructure, workshops, and community partnerships
- Connect with Somos El Cambio Foundation and other local regenerative organizations
- Estimated RT flight: $200-300

### Sequencing Strategy

1. **Costa Rica (late Feb)** -- Envision Festival as the opening storytelling activation. Capture regenerative event infrastructure as a model. Connect with aligned practitioners.

2. **Bogota (late Mar)** -- Transition directly from Costa Rica to Colombia for the Estereo Picnic / FITB cultural moment. Layer festival storytelling with visits to the 5 active bioregional sites. (Flights from San Jose to Bogota are affordable and direct.)

3. **Jamaica (late Nov)** -- Close the year with deep, relationship-focused storytelling in the Jamaica bioregion during the shoulder season. Document Rastafari Indigenous Village stewardship and post-hurricane recovery. Lower costs and quieter atmosphere allow for intimate, community-centered content.

This sequence follows the PPP cycle: **SENSE** (Jamaica -- listening, relationship), **SEE** (Colombia -- mapping the 5 active regions), **CELEBRATE** (Costa Rica Envision + Bogota festivals), and sets up **FLOW** and **REGENERATE** for the storytelling content to attract resources.
