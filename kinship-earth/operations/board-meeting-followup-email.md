## Summary of Ideas, Reflections & Feedback

### Storytelling & Film

- Create short films and YouTube content showing the full journey of flow funding — from donor to flow funder to recipient community. Visual storytelling is the most powerful way to attract support and spread the model. *(Msrion, Terry, Eslerh, Doc)*
- Visit bioregions in person to capture authentic, high-quality stories — the feeling, sound, and texture of place can't be conveyed on paper. *(Eslerh)*
- Tell the flow funder's story too — being entrusted with funds is transformative for the giver, not just the recipient. That learning journey matters. *(Susanna, Marion)*
- Marion: *"If you use visuals... people will respond if they can feel it."*

### Fundraising & Funder Engagement

- Sheri has a meeting with a billionaire-class family office from Mexico — a young man eager to learn where to deploy funds. She'll track what language and framing resonates and report back.
- Language matters — the group acknowledged the real difficulty of translating flow funding into terms institutional funders understand. It's *"a different way of seeing, a different way of being."* *(Marion)*
- Each funder conversation is a mini case study — track what worked, what language landed, what they wanted to hear. *(Sheri)*

### New Revenue & Structural Ideas

- **For-profit merchandising arm** — Kinship Earth branded products sold online, generating income and brand awareness. *(Stephen)*
- **"Kinship Earth Global Transformation Corps"** — a Peace Corps-style cadre of people working on bioregional projects worldwide. *(Stephen)*
- **Venture philanthropy** — fund projects with sustainable business models (tinctures, organic products, etc.) so they become self-sustaining rather than donation-dependent. *(Susanna)*
- **AI for fund allocation** — use AI to synthesize information and identify where funds should flow most effectively, without replacing the trust-based process. *(Susanna)*

### Philosophy & Framing

- Flow funding as democratizing philanthropy — shifting the power of discernment from billionaires to people on the ground. *"Why should just my eyes be seeing what's important?"* *(Marion)*
- Harmonize making and giving — how money is earned often contradicts how it's given away. The aspiration is a unified, regenerative cycle. *(Marion)*
- Recognize wealth beyond dollars — quantify the true environmental cost of destruction and the inherent value of nature. Flow funds don't have to be limited to money. *(Dana)*
- Building something that outlasts us all — the most important thing is creating an organization that will endure. *(Terry)*
- Terry framed the current moment as an opportunity: the contrast between immature, self-interest-driven systems and the common-good values this group holds makes it easier to present the alternative.

### Networks & Movement Building

- Track and connect networks of networks — permaculture, Communities of Compassion, Planetary Party Protocol, etc. Don't build new things; weave what exists. *(Sharon Joy)*
- Kinship as worldview — the shift from fragmentation to kinship/connection is the deeper story. Flow funding is one expression of it. *(Sheri)*
- Sheri's framing: *"We are the harbingers of the new planetary operating system."*

### Culture & Gathering

- Doc's **"Day of Caring"** concept — when flowing funds to communities, frame it as a day of caring/sharing. Could become a global movement.
- Be the light — Doc emphasized that consistently putting good into the world overshadows the chaos. *"People will catch on because they're going to get tired of the snow."*
- One flow funder at a time — Marion counseled against getting overwhelmed by the grand vision. *"Like monks walking, one step at a time. Before you know it, we get to Washington, D.C."*
- Song from Marion: *"You can't take a suitcase to heaven... no room for money up above, the only place there is is for all of your love."*
