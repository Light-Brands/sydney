## 2. Earth Bridge Community Land Trust (EBCLT)

This is directly relevant to Unified Sanctuaries given your own CLT model.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Based in** | Putney, VT (P.O. Box 553, Putney, VT 05346) |
| **Total land** | ~400 acres across 7 parcels in Windham County, VT and Cheshire County, NH |
| **Members** | ~50 land users on 22 long-term leaseholds |
| **Founded** | One of the oldest and largest community land trusts in the country |
| **Website** | [ebclt.org](https://www.ebclt.org) |

### EBCLT Lease Rules (relevant to what you could/couldn't do)

- **Primary residence required** -- leaseholds cannot be second homes, vacation properties, or rentals
- **Subletting or absences >1 year** require special Board approval
- **Board consent required** before: new construction, expanding structures, installing solar, logging/clearing, or beginning agricultural endeavors
- **Land users own their buildings** but lease the land
- **Lease fees below market value**, minimal down payment, no interest, 10-20 year repayment
- **Home sale prices capped** at 100% of appraised value (some voluntarily accept less)
- **No extractive practices** (mining etc.) allowed
- **Stewardship-first culture** -- EBCLT selects members who share their values

### Implications for Unified Sanctuaries
- BEEC's arrangement with EBCLT is a **precedent and potential model** for how Unified Sanctuaries' land trust could structure leases with operating entities
- If you were interested in this specific property, you'd need to work through EBCLT's transfer process -- they guide it "from start to finish"
- The primary residence requirement and Board consent for changes would need to be navigated for any community living or event venue use
- EBCLT's values (ecological stewardship, community, alternative land ownership) are **highly aligned** with Unified Sanctuaries

## 4. Traffic & Visitor Volume

### Bonnyvale Road Traffic
- Bonnyvale Road is a **local/town road** (portions are gravel) -- not a state route
- **No published AADT (Annual Average Daily Traffic) data found** in VTrans databases -- local roads are often not counted
- As a rural road 2.2 miles off Western Ave (Route 9), traffic is expected to be **very low** -- likely in the range of a few hundred vehicles per day at most
- The road has a "very steep hill" (Danny's Nub) approaching BEEC, which naturally limits speed and throughput

### BEEC Visitor Volume
- No published annual attendance numbers found
- BEEC runs: weekly playgroups, afterschool programs, school field trips, weekend family adventures, day camps, public walks/talks, workshops, and an annual gathering
- Trails are open dawn to dusk, free to the public
- Small parking area suggests **modest peak capacity** -- likely dozens, not hundreds, of visitors at a time
- For a $514K/year nonprofit with 15 staff, visitor volume is likely in the **low thousands annually**

### Traffic Implications for Unified Sanctuaries
- **Low baseline traffic is both an opportunity and a constraint** -- rural character is preserved, but event traffic (festivals, weddings, concerts) would be highly noticeable on a road this quiet
- Any significant increase in traffic would likely require:
  - Traffic impact study
  - Road improvement assessment (gravel road, steep hill)
  - Parking plan beyond what exists
  - Potential neighbor/community engagement process
- VTrans traffic data portal: [vtrans.vermont.gov/operations/OSB/data/traffic](https://vtrans.vermont.gov/operations/OSB/data/traffic)

## 5. Zoning -- Brattleboro Land Use Regulations

### Likely Zoning District

The BEEC property at 1221 Bonnyvale Road (2.2 miles from Route 9, deep into rural West Brattleboro) most likely falls in the **Rural Residential (RR)** or **Rural (RL)** district.

- The Village Center district around West Brattleboro extends south to Bonnyvale Road near the Route 9 junction, but the BEEC property is well beyond that boundary
- The area has "decidedly rural character, with dirt roads, sparse housing, wooded Green Mountains foothills"
- To confirm the exact district, use the [Brattleboro online GIS map](https://www.mapsonline.net/brattleborovt/index.html) or the [Vermont Open Geodata Portal zoning layer](https://geodata.vermont.gov/datasets/windhamregional::vt-data-brattleboro-zoning/explore)

### Current Regulations (Effective August 15, 2023)

Brattleboro's **Land Use and Development Regulations** govern all development. Key provisions:

| Topic | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Governing document** | Land Use and Development Regulations (adopted 2023) |
| **Rural Residential (RR)** | Chapter 230, Section 231, Use Table Figure 2-23 |
| **Rural (RL)** | Chapter 230, Section 232 |
| **Full PDF** | [Windham Regional Commission](https://windhamregional.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Brattleboro_zoningbylaw_2023-07-25.pdf) |
| **Contact** | Brattleboro Planning Dept: 802-251-8154 |
| **Development Review Board** | Reviews conditional uses, site plans, subdivisions |
| **Town zoning page** | [brattleboro.gov/zoning](https://brattleboro.gov/zoning) |

### Key Zoning Considerations for Unified Sanctuaries

Based on what's known about the RR and RL districts:

**Likely Permitted (verify against Figure 2-23):**
- Single-family residential
- Agriculture / farming
- Forestry
- Home occupations
- Low-density residential development

**Likely Conditional Use (requires DRB review):**
- Educational facilities / community centers
- Inns / lodging
- Cottage clusters (tiny home villages) -- explicitly noted as allowed in RR
- Retreats / wellness centers
- Event venues

**Likely Prohibited or Restricted:**
- High-density residential
- Commercial/retail (beyond farm stand / home occupation scale)
- Industrial uses
- Therapeutic community residences (explicitly noted as NOT allowed in RR)

### Special Overlay Districts
- **Flood Hazard Area (SFHA):** If property is in a flood zone, ALL improvements (even interior) need permits. Check FEMA maps.
- **Historic Resource Overlay:** If applicable, exterior work needs design review.
- **Missing Middle Housing Overlay:** Brattleboro has adopted provisions to allow missing middle housing types with simplified permitting.

### Vermont State-Level Rules
- **Parking:** In districts served by municipal sewer/water that allow residential uses, max 1 parking space per dwelling can be required. In areas NOT served by sewer/water (like Bonnyvale Rd), municipalities may require up to 1.5 spaces per unit.
- **Act 250:** Vermont's statewide land use review. Projects above certain thresholds (typically 10+ units residential, or significant commercial development) require an Act 250 permit from the District Environmental Commission. This is a **major regulatory layer** for Unified Sanctuaries.
- **Density caps removed:** Brattleboro passed an emergency bylaw amendment removing density caps, part of the "Zoning for Great Neighborhoods" initiative.

## 6. What You'd Need to Confirm / Action Items

### Before proceeding with BEEC or any Bonnyvale Road property:

1. **Confirm exact zoning district** -- Use the [Brattleboro GIS map tool](https://www.mapsonline.net/brattleborovt/index.html) to look up the parcel at 1221 Bonnyvale Road
2. **Review Figure 2-23 (RR Use Table)** -- Download the [full zoning bylaw PDF](https://windhamregional.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Brattleboro_zoningbylaw_2023-07-25.pdf) and check which of your four pillars are permitted vs. conditional vs. prohibited
3. **Contact Brattleboro Planning Department** (802-251-8154) for a pre-application conversation about:
   - Feasibility of a multi-use regenerative village in the RR/RL district
   - Conditional use requirements for retreats, events, community living
   - Subdivision requirements for 20+ household neighborhood
   - Road/infrastructure capacity on Bonnyvale Road
4. **Research Act 250 applicability** -- At the scale of Unified Sanctuaries ($10M+ development, 20+ homes, event venue, commercial operations), an Act 250 permit is almost certainly required
5. **Engage EBCLT** if the BEEC property specifically is of interest -- understand their process, timeline, and whether they'd entertain a project of this scope
6. **Commission a traffic impact study** -- Essential for any event venue or high-traffic use on a rural gravel road
7. **Check water/wastewater** -- The property is almost certainly on well water and septic. Multi-building, multi-household development would need engineering for water supply and wastewater treatment.
8. **Survey neighbors and community** -- West Brattleboro has an active community identity. Early engagement will be important.
