## 1. Institutional Overview

### The Opportunity

Unified Sanctuaries is a regenerative village planned for Southern Vermont (Brattleboro / Guilford / Putney corridor) that integrates permaculture farming, healing arts, cultural programming, and cooperative living into a single, self-sustaining community. The project is designed to acquire and transform a small university or college campus -- repurposing existing institutional infrastructure into a functioning village economy.

Across the Northeast, small college campuses are closing or consolidating, leaving behind tens of millions of dollars in built infrastructure: dining halls, dormitories, classrooms, performance spaces, agricultural land, gathering halls, workshops, and utilities already built to code. Unified Sanctuaries is positioned to acquire one of these campuses and activate it immediately.

### Why This Matters to Institutional Capital

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Asset Class** | Regenerative real estate / community land trust |
| **Geography** | Southern Vermont -- high quality of life, growing regenerative economy, strong agricultural heritage |
| **Infrastructure Advantage** | Campus acquisition eliminates 3-5 year buildout risk; revenue within 90 days of acquisition |
| **Revenue Model** | Four independent, diversified pillars generating $1.4M-$4.5M+ annually at maturity |
| **Exit Protection** | Land held in perpetual Community Land Trust -- mission lock prevents speculative extraction |
| **Replication Value** | Open-source blueprint designed for global adaptation -- investment catalyzes a movement, not just a property |

## 2. Entity & Legal Architecture

### 2.1 Corporate Structure

```
UNIFIED SANCTUARIES COMMUNITY LAND TRUST
(Vermont Nonprofit -- Holds Land in Perpetuity)
                |
    +-----------+-----------+
    |           |           |
    |     ANCHOR TRUST      |
    |   (Stewardship &      |
    |    Coordination)      |
    |                       |
    +---+---+---+---+-------+
        |   |   |   |
        v   v   v   v
    FARM  RETREAT  EVENT  COMMUNITY
    TRUST  TRUST   TRUST  LIVING TRUST
    (Pillar 1) (Pillar 2) (Pillar 3) (Pillar 4)
```

**Community Land Trust (CLT):** Owns and stewards the land in perpetuity, keeping it outside market speculation. Non-operating entity -- its role is asset protection and long-term stewardship.

**Anchor Trust:** Coordinates shared infrastructure, stewards pooled funds, and maintains governance coherence across pillars.

**Pillar Trusts (4):** Each core pillar operates through its own trust with autonomy over internal operations, compensation, and programming -- while maintaining accountability to the shared governance framework and land trust agreements.

### 2.3 Fiscal & Tax Strategy

- **Property Tax:** Vermont CLTs may qualify for Current Use (Act 183) valuation, reducing property taxes by 80-90% on agricultural and forestland
- **Sales Tax:** Vermont exempts agricultural sales under $10,000 and most educational services
- **Income Tax:** Revenue generated within pillar trusts is subject to Vermont business entity tax; nonprofit CLT revenue from mission-aligned activities may be tax-exempt
- **Charitable Deductions:** Donations to the CLT (if 501(c)(3)) are tax-deductible; land conservation easements may qualify for enhanced deductions
- **Investment Structure:** Impact investment vehicles structured as revenue-linked instruments avoid equity complications and securities risks when properly structured under Regulation D or Regulation CF

### 7.2 Impact Investment Structure

Investment in Unified Sanctuaries is structured through commons-aligned instruments that generate returns without conferring ownership or governance control over community assets.

**Revenue-Linked Notes:**
- **Principal:** $100,000 minimum investment
- **Term:** 7-10 years
- **Return Structure:** Revenue share of 5-15% of gross revenue, capped at 2x principal
- **Priority:** Investor returns paid before surplus distributions
- **Governance:** No voting rights, no board seats, no decision-making power over community governance
- **Reporting:** Quarterly financial dashboard, annual impact report, annual independent audit (beginning Year 2)
- **Mission Lock:** Commons-aligned trust wrapper prevents mission drift or speculative extraction

**Community Notes (Regulation CF):**
- **Minimum Investment:** $250
- **Maximum Raise:** $5,000,000 per 12-month period
- **Return:** Fixed interest (3-5%) + revenue kicker
- **Term:** 5-7 years
- **Purpose:** Broad community ownership and participation
