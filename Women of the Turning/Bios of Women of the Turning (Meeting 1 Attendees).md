### **Dr. Nina Meyerhof, Ed.D.**

Dr. Nina Meyerhof is an educator, peacebuilder, and global youth advocate whose life’s work has been devoted to awakening inner leadership in young people and advancing a culture of unity across the world. As President and Founder of Children of the Earth and Founder of the One Humanity Institute, she is recognized internationally as a visionary thought leader dedicated to the realization of global peace through education and conscious action. 

With a doctorate in Educational Policy, Research, and Administration, along with advanced degrees in Special Education, Counseling, and Psychology, Dr. Meyerhof has spent decades designing innovative educational frameworks rooted in authentic learning and global responsibility. 

 She is co-author of Conscious Education: The Bridge to Freedom and Pioneering Spiritual Activism, works that bridge personal development, ethical leadership, and societal transformation.

Long before her work reached more than 80 countries, Nina was cultivating peace close to home. For 30 years, she founded and stewarded Heart’s Bend, a values-oriented children’s summer camp on her Vermont farm \- an immersive learning community devoted to self-esteem, cooperation, creativity, and ethical living. This intimate, land-based commitment to nurturing young people forms the living foundation beneath her global initiatives.

Through Children of the Earth, Dr. Meyerhof developed the globally recognized Reflect–Connect–Act model, a process integrating spiritual growth with meaningful societal engagement. Her pioneering constructs of Conflict-Transcendence \- moving beyond resolution and transformation toward deeper unity \- and Lateral Leadership Governance have informed collaborative leadership practices across cultures. 

Children of the Earth has reached young people in over 80 countries and fostered approximately 30 youth hubs and chapters worldwide, supporting projects that build bridges across cultural, religious, and national divides. 

 From establishing the first Children’s Peace Center in Ghana to youth-led initiatives in Nepal, the Middle East, Eastern Europe, and beyond, her work centers on the understanding that peace begins with recognizing our interwoven unity.

Dr. Meyerhof’s lifetime of service has been honored with numerous awards, including the Mother Teresa Award, the Citizens Department of Peace Award, the International Educators Award for Peace, and a State of Vermont Resolution recognizing her life’s work in peacebuilding. 

 She serves as a Council member of Evolutionary Leaders and continues to convene youth and adults worldwide in dialogue, education, and collaborative action. 

Her current opus initiative, the One Humanity Institute \- A City of Hope \- emerges adjacent to Auschwitz as a living educational complex dedicated to transforming “Never Again” into “Ever Again.” 

 This initiative embodies the movement from Hate to Hope, from I to We to One, offering structured learning experiences for youth and adults committed to resolving conflict and inspiring alternative possibilities for our collective future.

At the heart of Dr. Meyerhof’s work is a simple yet profound conviction: humanity is one global family. Through education, ethical living, and conscious collaboration, she invites future generations to embody the unity our world so urgently requires.

### **Grandmother Dr. Anita Sanchez**

Grandmother Dr. Anita L. Sanchez is a Nahua (Aztec) and Toltec wisdom keeper, organizational psychologist, author, and global leadership guide who has lived and worked in sacred relationship with Indigenous elders for more than four decades. With a Ph.D. in Organizational Psychology, she bridges ancestral teachings and contemporary leadership systems, guiding leaders, organizations, and communities through times of fragmentation, change, and renewal.

She is the author of The Four Sacred Gifts: Indigenous Wisdom for Modern Times (Simon & Schuster), a sacred transmission entrusted to her by a global collective of Indigenous elders. The Four Sacred Gifts \- Forgiveness, Unity, Healing, and Hope \- offer a living framework for personal and collective transformation in this era of profound transition. Rooted in the understanding that we are all one relation, her work calls humanity back into right relationship with one another and with the Earth.

For nearly four decades, Grandmother Anita has woven Indigenous wisdom, science, and business acumen into her consulting, facilitation, and speaking with Fortune 100 companies, nonprofit organizations, global networks, and intergenerational communities. She supports the cultivation of trust-based systems, cultures of belonging, and values-aligned leadership grounded not in dominance or extraction, but in reciprocity, coherence, and shared responsibility.

Her commitment to living wisdom extends beyond boardrooms and stages. Through her longstanding partnership with the Pachamama Alliance, she guides journeys into the Amazon rainforest, supporting leaders and families in deepening their relationship with Spirit, Earth, and Indigenous communities, while cultivating reverence for the protection and preservation of ancestral lands and cultures.

She serves as a member of the Transformational Leadership Council and Evolutionary Leaders, and offers her guidance through board and elders’ council roles with Kinship Earth, Bioneers, Earthwise Centre, Wisdom Weavers, Fire Circle Earth, the Well Being Project, and the Earth Rise Collective.

Whether addressing thousands from a stage, guiding executive teams through cultural transformation, walking in ceremony in the Amazon, or sitting in circle with elders, Grandmother Anita carries a steady, compassionate authority rooted in lived wisdom. Her life’s work invites us to heal the wounds of separation, reclaim sacred responsibility, and embody courageous, compassionate stewardship for the well-being of all life.

### **Alison Burchell** 

Alison is a climate and crustal-evolution geologist whose work bridges deep Earth science, public policy, and regenerative systems design. With dual bachelor’s degrees in Chemistry and Environmental Planning and graduate degrees in Volcanology and Isotope Geochemistry, her career integrates rigorous scientific inquiry with civic engagement and ecological advocacy.

Following a Fellowship with the U.S. Department of the Interior and the U.S. Geological Survey, Alison established a consulting practice focused on crustal processes, reef and wetland preservation, land reclamation, renewable energy siting, bio-geo-mimicry, and stakeholder facilitation. Her work examines the mechanisms and kinetics by which carbon is naturally removed from the atmosphere and stored in terrestrial systems, advancing integrative approaches to natural carbon sequestration and ecological resilience.

Alison has co-drafted legislation and successfully advocated for public health, environmental protection, endangered species preservation, regenerative agriculture, renewable energy, and energy storage initiatives. She has served on international, state, and local policy working groups and nonprofit boards advancing climate action, environmental education, and community-based energy transition.

As co-founder of Clean Energy Action (501c3) and Empower Our Future (501c4), she helped build broad coalitions of citizens, businesses, elected officials, and organizations to support communities in evaluating and implementing clean energy solutions. She is a member of the U.S. Climate Action Network, the World Forum, Colorado Citizens and Legislators for Climate Action, and has served on the City of Boulder’s Energy Future Task Force.

In her role as a board member and advisor to clean energy and sustainability ‘think-dream-do tanks’, she mentors youth leaders, community groups, and policymakers in advancing climate-forward, renewable energy, and biodiversity-restoring strategies.. Her approach \- what she calls Collaborative Impact \- integrates scientific rigor with inclusive stakeholder engagement to support communities, businesses, and governments in achieving durable sustainability goals.

Her advocacy is grounded in a simple truth: Earth’s climate system, which has sustained the miracle of life, is being transformed by human activity. With urgency and clarity, she works to strengthen the foundational relationships between air, land, water, wildlife, Indigenous wisdom, and informed civic action, so that future generations inherit a thriving planet.

She speaks, at least conversationally, Spanish, French, German, and Earth \- and is learning the languages of cat, bee, tree, bird, bunny, whale, and dream-time.
